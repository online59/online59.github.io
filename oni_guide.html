OXYGEN NOT INCLUDED — BASE GAME (VANILLA) SURVIVAL & BUILD GUIDE

Focus: Main game only (NOT the Spaced Out! DLC) | Date: 2026-06-15

NOTE ON "LEVELS": ONI has no XP levels. Progress is measured by CYCLES (in-game day counter) and RESEARCH TIERS (tech tree). "Which level to build" = which research tier unlocks it.

1. Game Setup & Starting Duplicants ^ top

Recommended for a first/safe playthrough

  • Asteroid: TERRA (standard balanced starting world)
  • Mode: "No Sweat" (stress builds slower, more forgiving)
  • You start with 3 Duplicants.

Choosing your 3 starting Duplicants

You do NOT pick a single named hero — you pick 3 randomly-generated Duplicants and can re-roll them. Two proven openings:

  • Option A (recommended): 1 Digger + 1 Builder + 1 Researcher
  • Option B (faster start): 2 Diggers + 1 Researcher

Pick by INTEREST — an interest makes matching skills effectively free in morale and speeds the work:

  • One Dupe interested in DIGGING (Excavation) → gathers all your materials
  • One Dupe interested in RESEARCH (Science) → unlocks the tech tree fast
  • One Dupe interested in BUILDING or FARMING → construction / food

A Dupe with an interest gets -1 effective morale cost on those skills (Tier 1 skills become basically free) AND completes that work type faster, with -1 stress.

2. Negative Traits to Avoid ^ top

Avoid at all costs (early-game colony killers)

  • Anemic — very low Athletics; painfully slow movement
  • Mouth Breather — consumes far more oxygen
  • Bottomless Stomach — eats ~50% more food (1,500 kcal/day vs 1,000)
  • Flatulent — randomly emits Natural Gas into your base
  • Loud Sleeper — wakes nearby Dupes, wrecking sleep/morale
  • Narcolepsy — falls asleep anywhere, mid-task

Good/harmless traits to keep: high base Athletics/Strength, "interests" that match your needed jobs, traits with downsides you can ignore early.

3. Research / Tech Tree Priority ^ top

Build a Research Station EARLY (cycle 1-2). Research order, top priority first:

  1. FOOD branch first — "Farming" → unlocks Planter Box (grow Mealwood) and Liceloaf. Starting rations run out ~cycle 6.
  2. POWER / "Power Regulation" — extends generator/wire capacity; needed before Coal generators scale.
  3. LIQUID & GAS PLUMBING / "Plumbing" + "Sanitation" — Pipes, Gas/Liquid Pumps, Lavatory, Water Sieve → closes water and toilet loops.
  4. ADVANCED RESEARCH (Super Computer) — unlocks Electrolyzer (→ SPOM), Algae/oxygen tech, automation, ranching, refinement.
  5. RANCHING (mid-game) — more efficient than pure farming; gives derived products. Pursue once survival basics are stable.
  6. AUTOMATION / REFINEMENT — automation wires, Metal Refinery, temperature management. Mid/late game.

General rule: Food + simple Power first, then Plumbing/Gas to move fluids, then Advanced systems as the colony grows.

3.1 Complete Research Route (Step by Step) ^ top

Build a RESEARCH STATION on cycle 1. Around cycle 10-15 build a SUPER COMPUTER to unlock advanced (green) techs. Research ONE branch at a time — half-researched branches give you nothing.

P0-P1 (Cycles 0-3) — Survival

  1. BASIC FARMING — Planter Box, Microbe Musher. THE #1 first pick — starting rations run out ~cycle 6.
  2. POWER REGULATION — Battery, Power Transformer. Store manual generator's power and prep the backbone+transformer grid.

P2 (Cycles 3-10) — Food + Move Fluids

  1. FARM STATION / AGRICULTURE — Farm Tile + fertilization.
  2. VENTILATION — Gas Pump, Gas Pipe, Gas Vent, Deodorizer/Air Filter.
  3. PLUMBING — Liquid Pump, Liquid Pipe, Liquid Vent, Bottle Emptier.
  4. SANITATION — Lavatory, Sink, Water Sieve. Closes the water + toilet loop.

P3 (Cycles 10-30) — Close Loops, Scale Power, Prep the SPOM

(Build the SUPER COMPUTER early in this phase.)

  1. COMBUSTION — Coal Generator. First scalable power; pair with batteries.
  2. ADVANCED POWER REGULATION — Smart Battery, Conductive Wire, Heavi-Watt Wire, Power Shutoff.
  3. LIQUID/IMPROVED PLUMBING — Liquid Reservoir, valves, liquid sensors.
  4. IMPROVED VENTILATION — Gas Filter, Gas Reservoir, Insulated Gas Pipe, gas sensors.
  5. ELECTROLYZER (tech) — Electrolyzer. The core of the SPOM.
  6. RENEWABLE ENERGY — Hydrogen Generator. Research this BEFORE building the SPOM.

P4 (~Cycle 30) — Build the SPOM

No new tech strictly required if 10-12 are done. You now have Electrolyzer + Gas Pumps + Gas Filter + Hydrogen Generator + Insulated Gas Pipe. Build the SPOM (Section 6).

P5 (Cycles 30-50) — Comfort / Morale

  1. INTERIOR DECOR — Decor items, art, flower pots. Hit Great Hall's decor 20+ requirement.
  2. FOOD QUALITY / REJUVENATION — Refrigerator, Electric Grill / Gas Range.
  3. PLUMBING COMFORT — Shower + bathroom fittings.
  4. MEDICINE (I) — Apothecary / basic meds.

P6 (Cycles 50-100) — Automate, Ranch, Refine, Cool, Expand

  1. RANCHING — Grooming Station, Critter Drop-Off, Incubator.
  2. AUTOMATION — Automation Wire, Atmo/Hydro/Thermo Sensors.
  3. ADVANCED AUTOMATION — Logic Gates (AND/OR/NOT/Buffer/Filter), Automation Ribbon.
  4. SMELTING / REFINEMENT — Rock Crusher, Kiln, Metal Refinery.
  5. TEMPERATURE MODULATION — Thermo Sensor, Liquid Tepidizer, Thermo Aquatuner.
  6. INSULATION — Insulated Tile, Ceramic.
  7. (Optional, late) Solar / Natural Gas power, Rocketry/Space.

One-line summary: Farming → Power → Ventilation → Plumbing → Sanitation → (Super Computer) → Coal/Advanced Power → Improved Plumbing/Ventilation → Electrolyzer + Hydrogen Gen (SPOM) → Decor/Food/Showers → Ranching/Automation/Refinement/Cooling/Insulation.

4. Cycle-by-Cycle Build Timeline ^ top

The three survival problems to solve first: TOILETS, FOOD, OXYGEN.

Cycles 1-3 (Immediate Survival)

  • [ ] Dig out a starting living area (avoid breaching biome walls early)
  • [ ] OUTHOUSE x1-2 (toilet; first priority)
  • [ ] WASH BASIN (next to toilets)
  • [ ] COTS, one per Dupe (sleep restores stress)
  • [ ] RESEARCH STATION (start the tech tree immediately)
  • [ ] MANUAL GENERATOR + BATTERY (~400W while running)
  • [ ] OXYGEN DIFFUSER x1-2 (early O2 from Algae)
  • [ ] STORAGE BIN x1-2
  • Research target: Farming

Cycles 3-10 (Food + Stability)

  • [ ] PLANTER BOXES + MEALWOOD → steady food
  • [ ] MICROBE MUSHER (cook Liceloaf)
  • [ ] PITCHER PUMP (manually collect water)
  • [ ] Keep saving WILD PLANTS (Muckroot, Meal Lice, Bristle Berry = free food)
  • [ ] More OXYGEN DIFFUSERS / ALGAE TERRARIUM as Dupe count grows
  • [ ] MESS HALL (simple dining room w/ Mess Tables) → easy early morale
  • Research target: Power Regulation, then Plumbing/Sanitation

Cycles 10-30 (Close the Loops, Scale Power)

  • [ ] LAVATORY + plumbing (replace Outhouses; recycle water)
  • [ ] WATER SIEVE (polluted water → usable water)
  • [ ] GAS / LIQUID PUMPS + PIPES
  • [ ] COAL GENERATORS (AWAY from living areas — they make heat!)
  • [ ] SMART BATTERY to switch coal gens on/off, save coal
  • [ ] BARRACKS (cots enclosed in a room) for bonus stamina
  • [ ] SUPER COMPUTER (unlocks Electrolyzer + advanced tech)
  • Research target: Advanced Research toward Electrolyzer

Around Cycle 30 (The Big One)

  • [ ] BUILD A SPOM (Self-Powered Oxygen Machine). This replaces Algae oxygen for good. See Section 6. Target: running by ~cycle 30.

Cycles 30-50 (Comfort + Morale)

  • [ ] GREAT HALL (upgrade Mess Hall) → big morale boost
  • [ ] RECREATION ROOM + recreation buildings
  • [ ] KITCHEN with proper cooking (better food → better morale)
  • [ ] WASHROOM / Showers, decor items to raise room decor 20+

Cycles 50-100 (Automation & Expansion)

  • [ ] Fully automated COAL power (Smart Batteries)
  • [ ] Expand farms, add a second oxygen source / bigger SPOM
  • [ ] Start RANCHING (Hatches for coal + meat)
  • [ ] METAL REFINERY, temperature management (insulated tiles, cooling)
  • [ ] Seal biome boundaries with INSULATED TILES + AIRLOCKS

5. Base Layout / Construction Design ^ top

5.1 Core Geometry Rules ^ top

  • Make main rooms and hallways 4 TILES HIGH (fits decor/lights; most buildings are 1-3 tall)
  • Use 3-tile-wide vertical ladder/transit shafts: 1 ladder + Fire Pole + gap
  • When digging vertical shafts, leave 1-2 empty tiles on each side of the ladder for gas circulation and Dupe pathing
  • Minimize ladders early — Dupes are slow on ladders. Add FIRE POLES next to ladders (poles only go down)
  • Build wide rather than tall early; tall bases punish low-Athletics Dupes
  • Make spacious room entrances so traffic doesn't bottleneck

5.2 Vertical Zoning — Where Each Room Goes ^ top

Think in horizontal floors stacked around a central ladder/pole spine. Hot/dirty stuff goes far out to sides or down; oxygen sources go above so fresh O2 falls into rooms.

Top Floors (low traffic)

  • RESEARCH (Research Station / Super Computer)
  • SPOM / oxygen production & hydrogen handling — put oxygen sources ABOVE so cool O2 sinks down
  • High-tech parts STORAGE

Middle Floors (the living HUB — cluster buildings, same level)

  • GREAT HALL / MESS HALL (eating = morale; central so everyone reaches it)
  • KITCHEN / Electric Grill + Refrigerator (adjacent to hall)
  • RECREATION ROOM (near the hall)
  • BEDROOM / BARRACKS (adjacent but SEALED off from noise & traffic)

Lower-Mid Floors

  • REFRIGERATED FOOD + mineral/material STORAGE
  • WASHROOM block: TOILETS (Lavatory) + WASH BASIN/SINK + SHOWER

Bottom / Far-Side Floors (high heat, isolate behind insulated tiles)

  • POWER ROOM: Coal/other GENERATORS + BATTERIES
  • INDUSTRIAL: Metal Refinery, Kilns, heavy machinery
  • LIQUID/GAS plumbing reservoirs, Water Sieve, sewage processing

Community Standard: Floor Heights & Dimensions

The community consensus is 4 tiles high per functional room — this is the maximum height for most buildings and keeps everything reachable. Room widths: 16 tiles wide × 4 high = 64 tiles (fits Barracks, Latrine, Mess Hall) or 24 tiles wide × 4 high = 96 tiles (fits Power Plant, Greenhouse, Great Hall, Hospital).

Recommended: add a 2-tile utility gap between every floor. This gives a floor-to-floor pitch of 4 + 2 = 6 tiles total. All horizontal pipes, wires, pipe bridges, shutoffs, valves, and sensors run inside that gap — never inside the functional room above or below. This keeps rooms clean, hides decor-negative pipes from Dupes, and makes it far easier to add or modify lines later without touching room layouts. The vertical transit shaft (ladder + fire pole) passes straight through the gap tiles as normal.

VERTICAL FLOOR STACKING — 4-tile room + 2-tile utility gap (6-tile pitch)

  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |   ROOM (e.g. Barracks)                           |  row 4  ↑
  |   Buildings, Dupes, decor items live here        |  row 3  | 4 tiles
  |                                                  |  row 2  | (functional)
  |                                                  |  row 1  ↓
  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |  UTILITY GAP — top row: pipe runs, wire runs,    |  gap 2  ↑
  |    pipe bridges, shutoffs, valves, atmo sensors  |         | 2 tiles
  |  UTILITY GAP — bot row: ladders pass through     |  gap 1  ↓
  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |   ROOM (e.g. Mess Hall)                          |  row 4  ↑
  |                                                  |  row 3  | 4 tiles
  |                                                  |  row 2  |
  |                                                  |  row 1  ↓
  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |  UTILITY GAP — pipe/wire runs + tap points       |  gap 2  ↑ 2 tiles
  |  UTILITY GAP — ladders                           |  gap 1  ↓
  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |   ROOM (e.g. Power Room)                         |  rows 1-4 (4 tiles)
  +--------------------------------------------------+

WHAT GOES IN THE GAP:
  gap row 2 (upper): pipe bridges to cross multiple lines without T-junctions
                     horizontal liquid/gas pipe runs
                     standard/conductive wire runs
  gap row 1 (lower): SHUTOFF + VALVE + ATMO/LIQUID SENSOR for each tap
                     ladder segment (transit shaft passes straight through)

RULE: Horizontal utility lines ONLY run in the gap. Rooms above and below stay
      clear of pipes and wires except for the final 1-tile connection to each building.

Community Horizontal Layout Pattern (top-down floor view)

From Klei Entertainment forum and Steam discussions — the most widely used repeating pattern from left to right on each floor:

   ←—— 24 tiles ——→ ←3→ ←— 5 —→ ←— 16-24 tiles —→ ←3→ ←— 5 —→ ←—— 24 tiles ——→
   +----------------+   +-------+----------------+   +-------+----------------+
   |                |LAD|  MAINT|                |LAD|  MAINT|                |
   |  ROOM A        |POL|  ALCO |  ROOM B        |POL|  ALCO |  ROOM C        |
   |  (96 tiles)    |air|  VE   |  (64-96 tiles) |air|  VE   |  (96 tiles)    |
   |  e.g.          |   |       |  e.g.          |   |       |  e.g.          |
   |  Great Hall    |   |[XFMR] |  Barracks/     |   |[XFMR] |  Washroom /    |
   |  + Kitchen     |   |[PUMP] |  Bedroom       |   |[PUMP] |  Storage       |
   +----------------+---+-------+----------------+---+-------+----------------+
   ROOM A/C = 24×4 = 96 tiles (large room)
   ROOM B   = 16×4 = 64 tiles (standard room)
   SHAFT    = 3 tiles: 1 ladder + 1 fire pole + 1 open air gap
   ALCOVE   = 5 tiles: transformer + pump + dispenser + spare space (1 tile each side is wall)

The maintenance alcove (5 tiles wide) sits between a room and the shaft on each floor. It holds infrastructure — Power Transformer, pumps, dispensers — so those never clutter functional rooms. All floors repeat the same column positions, so risers run clean from top to bottom.

Community Gas Sorting Rule

Gases sort by density automatically. Design each floor so the right gas ends up where you want it without extra pumping:

   FLOOR     GAS PRESENT          DESIGN IMPLICATION
   ------    ---------            ------------------
   TOP       Hydrogen (rises)     → H2 generator / SPOM at top catches H2 passively
   UPPER     Oxygen (mid-weight)  → Bedrooms at upper-mid: O2 pools here naturally
   MIDDLE    Oxygen / Polluted O2 → Place airflow/mesh tiles in long rooms (every ~8 tiles)
                                    to let O2 reach far corners and CO2 drain down
   LOWER     Carbon Dioxide       → CO2 pump near the floor of lower floors
   BOTTOM    CO2 + Chlorine       → Chlorine storage sealed at bottom; CO2 skimmer low

Use Airflow Tiles (mesh floor tiles) in long rooms (20+ tiles) — place 2–3 along the floor so O2 can reach the far end and CO2 can fall through to the lower floor. Normal solid tiles block vertical gas movement.

5.3 Per-Building Placement Notes ^ top

Generators

  • In a SEALED power room FAR from bedrooms — heat, CO2, and noise
  • CO2 sinks → put a CO2 pump beneath or let it pool away from breathing height
  • Keep coal storage right next to the gens

Toilets (Outhouse early → Lavatory later)

  • Group in a WASHROOM. A 4x8 room with ~4 toilets + 4 sinks handles a small to medium crew
  • Put a WASH BASIN/SINK on the EXIT tile so Dupes auto-wash hands
  • Keep the washroom NEAR (but sealed from) the kitchen

Food Hall + Kitchen

  • Central so all Dupes path through it; eating in a Great Hall is a big morale source
  • KITCHEN + REFRIGERATOR adjacent so cooked food travels minimal distance to tables
  • Keep the kitchen germ-clean: don't share air with toilets or polluted water

Bedroom / Barracks

  • SEALED and QUIET: away from generators, industry, and busy corridors
  • Put oxygen production above so O2 settles into the sleeping area

Waste Water (Polluted Water)

  • Route toilet/shower output to a sealed polluted-water reservoir, then a Water Sieve turns it back to clean water
  • Sieve does NOT remove germs or heat — clean water keeps its germs
  • Keep polluted water sealed — it off-gasses Polluted Oxygen

5.4 Plumbing — Water & Gas Pipe Best Practices ^ top

General

  • Keep SUPPLY and DRAIN/return as two separate lines; never mix outputs of different liquids/gases
  • ONE SUBSTANCE = ONE PIPE
  • Flow direction at a junction is fixed — if water flows the "wrong" way, redesign the layout

Pipe Capacity

  • Liquid pipe: max 10 kg/s (very high headroom)
  • Gas pipe: max 1 kg/s (TIGHT; gas clogs far sooner)

Liquid Spines to Reserve (each carries 10 kg/s)

  1. CLEAN WATER (supply) → SPOM, farms, kitchen, showers, research. One spine is plenty early.
  2. POLLUTED WATER (drain) → from toilets/showers/sieve input. One spine.
  3. (future) CRUDE OIL / PETROLEUM
  4. (future) spare/coolant

Reserve ~3-4 liquid pipe tiles in the spine.

Gas Spines to Reserve (each carries only 1 kg/s)

  1. OXYGEN (distribution) — one spine per SPOM (~per 8 dupes)
  2. CARBON DIOXIDE (removal)
  3. HYDROGEN (usually consumed inside SPOM)
  4. (future) CHLORINE, NATURAL GAS, POLLUTED OXYGEN

Reserve ~5-6 gas pipe tiles in the spine.

Bridges

  • Flow is PRIORITIZED out the BRIDGE. Use to build self-regulating loops.
  • Use bridges to "jump over" a pipe so two lines can cross without joining

Valves & Shutoffs

  • LIQUID/GAS VALVE: cap flow to an exact rate
  • SHUTOFF + sensor: automated, instant on/off switch
  • FILTER: routes one chosen substance to one output

Gas Pipes

  • Vent gases ONLY into dedicated sealed rooms with a Gas Pump
  • Oxygen distribution: pump SPOM oxygen through pipes to gas vents placed HIGH in each room
  • Add Atmo Sensor + shutoff to stop at target pressure (~1.8-2.0 kg/tile)

Pipe Material / Heat

  • Normal pipes exchange heat with surroundings. Use INSULATED PIPES to preserve temperature.
  • Insulated pipe cuts heat transfer to ~3%. Best cheap material: IGNEOUS ROCK (recommended).

5.5 Power / Electrical Wiring Best Practices ^ top

Wire Capacities

  • Standard Wire: 1,000 W (cheap; default everywhere small)
  • Conductive Wire: 2,000 W (needs Refined Metal; no decor hit)
  • Heavi-Watt Wire: 20,000 W (-25 decor, 6-tile radius — must be hidden)
  • Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire: 50,000 W (-20 decor, 4-tile radius; Gold version: -10 decor)

The Standard "Backbone + Transformer" Pattern

  1. Run a HEAVI-WATT backbone from your generator/battery room through its own dedicated sealed corridor, completely separate from and not adjacent to the main transit shaft. Heavi-Watt CANNOT be embedded inside a solid tile — it must sit in an empty air cell. Wall off both sides of that cell so Dupes cannot path through or see it (the wide decor penalty must never reach bedrooms or work areas). To pass through a wall or obstacle, use a Heavi-Watt Joint Plate (placed in place of a tile to let the wire cross).
  2. At each room/zone, drop a POWER TRANSFORMER off the backbone. Small = 1,000 W output; Large = 4,000 W. Transformers are the ONLY intended way to separate circuits. Run a short standard wire from the transformer into the room — the Heavi-Watt backbone never enters living areas.
  3. On the consumer side, use cheap standard (1 kW) or conductive (2 kW) wire. Keep each branch's total load UNDER the transformer's output.

Community Pattern: Compact Power Spine (per-floor transformer layout)

Each floor taps power from the Heavi-Watt backbone via a Joint Plate and a Large Transformer. The backbone never enters any room — standard wire carries power from each transformer into the living area.

COMPACT POWER SPINE — community standard layout

  FLOOR n+2:  [ solid WALL ][ JOINT PLATE ][ HW wire ][ JOINT PLATE ][ solid WALL ]
                                                               |
                                                   [ Large Transformer 4kW ]
                                                               |
                                                 --1kW wire--> room circuit

  FLOOR n+1:  [ solid WALL ][ JOINT PLATE ][ HW wire ][ JOINT PLATE ][ solid WALL ]
                                                               |
                                                   [ Large Transformer 4kW ]
                                                               |
                                                 --1kW wire--> room circuit

  FLOOR n:    [ solid WALL ][ JOINT PLATE ][ HW wire ][ JOINT PLATE ][ solid WALL ]
                                                               |
                                                   [ Large Transformer 4kW ]
                                                               |
                                                 --1kW wire--> room circuit

              <----- sealed 1-tile Heavi-Watt corridor (Dupes cannot path here) ----->

Rules:
  - Joint Plate replaces one wall tile per floor so the backbone can exit to the transformer.
  - Transformer sits just outside the HW corridor (maintenance alcove or adjacent cell).
  - No Heavi-Watt wire ever enters the living area.
  - Small Transformer = 1 kW output (early base); Large Transformer = 4 kW (standard).
  - Keep total room draw UNDER the transformer output — add a second transformer if needed.

Batteries & Generator Control

  • Use SMART BATTERIES with automation: wire battery signal to generators so gens turn ON when batteries are low and OFF when full
  • Set thresholds ~5% (low) / ~99% (high) to avoid rapid on/off flicker

Overload Check & Failure

  • Watch the Power Overlay: white = fine, yellow = near limit, red = overloaded
  • WIRE BRIDGES break BEFORE wires do — put a bridge at an easy-to-reach spot
  • Split into zoned circuits via transformers so one heavy room can't brown out the rest

5.6 Environment / Sealing ^ top

  • Keep HEAT SOURCES (Coal gens, Metal Refinery, industry) FAR from the living base, behind INSULATED TILES
  • SEAL biome walls: don't breach neighboring biomes early — they hold dangerous gases, germs, and heat
  • Use Insulated Tiles + AIRLOCKS (Manual/Mechanized) as gas-tight doors between zones
  • Use ATMO SENSORS to keep base pressure steady and to gate oxygen vents

5.7 Routing the Spine — Vertical Risers + Horizontal Branches ^ top

Build ONE central vertical "utility column" beside the main ladder/fire-pole transit shaft. All risers (every liquid line, every gas line, the power backbone, automation wire) run UP/DOWN this column in parallel. On each floor, branch HORIZONTALLY off the column for the substances that floor needs.

Where the Column Goes

  • Put pipes INSIDE wall tiles flanking the ladder shaft (pipes, normal Wire, and Conductive Wire can be embedded in tiles)
  • HEAVI-WATT backbone is NOT part of the main utility column — it runs in its own separate sealed corridor elsewhere in the base (see 5.9). Never route it adjacent to the transit shaft or living areas.
  • Group lines: clean lines (clean water, oxygen) on one side; dirty lines (polluted water, CO2) on the other

Flow Direction in the Column

  • CLEAN WATER: source = sieve/reservoir at BOTTOM → rises UP → branches into SPOM (top), farms, kitchen, showers, research
  • OXYGEN: source = SPOM at TOP → falls DOWN → branches into every living floor
  • POLLUTED WATER: source = washroom (lower-mid) → drops DOWN → to Water Sieve at BOTTOM → output rejoins clean-water riser going back up
  • CARBON DIOXIDE: collected LOW → pumped DOWN/OUT to a pit or skimmer; never branch it up into living floors
  • POWER BACKBONE: source = power room at BOTTOM/FAR → rises UP → a Transformer taps off at each floor

Electrical Wire in the Column

5.9 Main Shaft & Utility Column — Tile-by-Tile Layout ^ top

Key game fact: Each map cell has separate utility layers — a single tile can simultaneously hold ONE liquid pipe + ONE gas pipe + ONE power wire + ONE automation wire. You only add another column when you need a SECOND liquid or SECOND gas.

HEAVI-WATT WIRE RULE: Heavi-Watt Wire and Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire cannot be embedded inside a solid tile and must not run adjacent to the main transit shaft or living areas. It carries a -25 decor penalty over a 6-tile radius — any Dupe who can see or path near it will suffer. Route it through a completely separate hidden corridor, sealed by wall tiles on both sides, with no Dupe access. Early base main shaft: 2 liquids (clean/polluted) + 3 gases (O2/CO2/H2) → 2 conduit wall columns flanking the 3-tile transit core. Power backbone lives elsewhere.

5.10 Tapping Utilities from the Main Column to Each Facility ^ top

The Universal Tap Order (riser → building, in this sequence):

RISER ==[BRIDGE]==> [SHUTOFF] -> [VALVE] -> [branch pipe] -> BUILDING
  • BRIDGE first, ON THE RISER: keeps the riser feeding downstream floors first; a backed-up branch can't dam the column
  • SHUTOFF next: lets you cut one facility off instantly without touching the riser
  • VALVE last: caps the branch to exactly what the building consumes

Per-Utility Tap Rules

  • LIQUID: tap with a bridge; building OUTPUT onto the correct return riser; use a valve to protect a low-draw machine
  • GAS: branch to a GAS VENT placed HIGH in the room; add ATMO SENSOR + SHUTOFF set to ~1.8-2.0 kg/tile; one vent per room is usually enough
  • POWER: drop a POWER TRANSFORMER off the Heavi-Watt backbone corridor at that floor; from the transformer output, run cheap 1k/2k wire into the room or into the main conduit column — the Heavi-Watt backbone itself never enters living areas
  • AUTOMATION: tap the signal line freely (no load limit); keep it on its OWN tile/layer

  • Bring ONLY the clean-water feed across the transit shaft (through the floor tile under the ladder)

5.11 Automation in the Base ^ top

Automation wire carries a GREEN (on) / RED (off) signal. It has no power load and no capacity limit — it is a completely separate layer from power wire. A single automation wire can run the full height of the base and branch to every floor with no penalty. The key is placing sensors next to the things they measure and keeping the signal wire on its own tile layer so it is never confused with power wire.

Automation Wire Routing in the Base

  • Automation wire CAN be embedded inside a solid wall tile (same as normal/conductive wire) — route it through the conduit wall columns alongside the pipes.
  • Run one automation riser in each conduit column (col1 dirty side, col6 clean side). Branch horizontally to each floor's sensors and shutoffs, the same way you branch pipes.
  • Keep automation wire on its own tile layer — do not share a cell with power wire. In the conduit walls, the layer stack is: liquid pipe + gas pipe + power wire + automation wire, all in one tile.
  • Color-code your mental model: automation signals flow FROM sensors TO the devices they control (pumps, shutoffs, generators). A sensor output that triggers multiple devices needs a splitter or you run a wire to each device in sequence.

Power Automation — Smart Battery + Generator Control

This is the #1 automation to build (Phase 3, when coal generators go in).

  • Smart Battery → placed in the power room beside generators. It has two signal outputs:
    • LOW threshold (~5% charge) → sends GREEN → wire to all generators → generators turn ON
    • HIGH threshold (~99% charge) → sends RED → generators turn OFF
  • Wire: Smart Battery output → automation riser in the Heavi-Watt corridor or a dedicated short run → each Generator's automation port.
  • Result: generators only burn fuel when the battery actually needs charging. A full colony can run for many cycles on one coal generator this way.
  • Use a BUFFER GATE on the signal if the generators flicker on/off rapidly (adds a time delay before switching, preventing wear cycles).
 [Smart Battery]--automation wire-->[Generator 1]
                 |
                 +-----------------> [Generator 2]
 Threshold LOW = ON (~5%) / HIGH = OFF (~99%)

Gas / Oxygen Automation — Atmo Sensor + Shutoff

Place one of these per room that receives piped oxygen (and on the SPOM's hydrogen handling).

  • Atmo Sensor — placed IN the room, reads the room's total gas pressure (kg/tile).
  • Set threshold: send GREEN (vent open) when pressure < ~1.8 kg/tile; send RED (vent closed) when pressure ≥ 2.0 kg/tile.
  • Wire: Atmo Sensor → Gas Pipe Shutoff on the branch that feeds that room's gas vent. When the room is full, the shutoff closes and the oxygen riser continues to the next floor without backing up.
  • Placement rule: put the Atmo Sensor away from the gas vent outlet (not directly next to it) so it reads the averaged room pressure, not a local spike from fresh gas.
  • CO2 removal: place an Atmo Sensor set to CO2 element filter near the floor of the room (CO2 sinks). Wire it to a gas pump shutoff — pump only runs when CO2 is present, saving power.

Liquid Automation — Hydro Sensor + Shutoff

  • Hydro Sensor — placed on a liquid pipe, reads the mass of liquid flowing past per second.
  • Common use 1 — Reservoir overflow protection: place a Hydro Sensor on the reservoir's output pipe. Set to send RED (pump off) when the reservoir is full (no flow = full). Wire to the input pump's shutoff so the pump stops when the tank is full.
  • Common use 2 — Water Sieve input: Hydro Sensor on the polluted-water feed pipe → if flow drops to 0 (reservoir empty), send RED to the sieve's power shutoff so it doesn't run dry and waste sand.
  • Common use 3 — SPOM water supply: Hydro Sensor before the Electrolyzer → if clean water stops flowing (reservoir empty), send RED to the SPOM's gas pump shutoff to avoid the electrolyzer running dry and breaking the gas separation.

SPOM Automation

A well-automated SPOM runs indefinitely without manual intervention. Minimum automation:

  • Atmo Sensor (inside SPOM room) — set to ~1.8 kg/tile minimum. Wire to both gas pumps. If pressure drops below this, the pumps run; if it rises above, they slow/stop. This keeps the H2 and O2 layers separated by pressure (they mix if pressure is too low).
  • Hydro Sensor on water input — if water feed is cut, shut off the gas pumps so the electrolyzer doesn't cavitate and the pump doesn't run empty.
  • Optional — Smart Battery inside SPOM: the Hydrogen Generator powers the machine. If you tie the Hydrogen Generator's automation port to the SPOM's own Smart Battery, the gen only fires when the internal battery is low, making the SPOM fully self-regulating.

Logic Gates — Combining Signals

Use logic gates when a device should only activate based on TWO or more conditions at once. Build these mid-game (Phase 6) once the basics are running.

  • AND gate — output GREEN only when ALL inputs are GREEN. Example: run a pump only when the reservoir is full AND it is the active day shift (AND a clock sensor + hydro sensor).
  • OR gate — output GREEN when ANY input is GREEN. Example: trigger an alert if either the oxygen pressure OR the CO2 level goes out of range.
  • NOT gate — inverts the signal. Example: Atmo Sensor sends GREEN when pressure is HIGH → NOT gate inverts it → shutoff receives RED (close) when full. Useful when a sensor's output logic is backwards from what the device expects.
  • BUFFER gate — delays a GREEN signal by N seconds before passing it on. Use on generator control to prevent rapid on/off flicker when the battery hovers near a threshold.
  • FILTER gate — passes a GREEN signal only if it stays GREEN for at least N seconds (ignores brief spikes). Use to stop a gas pump from pulsing on/off every second when pressure is borderline.
 Example — generator only runs when battery LOW and NOT night-time:
 [Smart Battery LOW]---\
                        AND gate ---> [Generator automation port]
 [Clock Sensor DAY] ---/

 Example — vent closes when room full (signal inversion):
 [Atmo Sensor HIGH pressure] -> [NOT gate] -> [Gas Shutoff]
 (sensor goes GREEN when full; NOT converts to RED = shutoff closes)

  • Phase 1-2: no automation needed; manual generator doesn't benefit from smart control yet.
  • Phase 3 (Cycle 10-30): Smart Battery → Coal Generator is the first and most impactful automation. Build it the moment the coal generator goes in. Also add Hydro Sensor on the Water Sieve feed.
  • Phase 4 (~Cycle 30, SPOM): Atmo Sensors inside the SPOM room + Hydro Sensor on the water feed. These are required for a stable SPOM — build them as part of the SPOM construction.
  • Phase 4-5: Atmo Sensor + Shutoff on every oxygen branch (one per room). Add as each room is connected to the O2 riser.
  • Phase 5-6: Thermo Sensor on heat sources (power room, refinery) → wire to cooling devices. Hydro Sensors on all reservoirs.
  • Phase 6: Logic gates for complex control (AND/OR/NOT/BUFFER/FILTER). Add only when a specific runaway problem exists that simpler sensors can't solve alone.

5.12 Room Building Guide ^ top

Rooms unlock bonuses only when ALL required buildings are present and the tile count is within the valid range. A room can only be ONE type — plan placement so buildings don't bleed across room boundaries.

Room Rules (Universal)

  • Tile count: counts every floor, wall, and ceiling tile enclosed within the room. Doors count as floor tiles. The room boundary is defined by solid tiles or doors — open space breaks it.
  • Room exclusivity: one room = one type. A Mess Table + Cot in the same room gives neither bonus. Keep room functions strictly separate.
  • Decor is evaluated from Dupe standing position — high-decor items on the ceiling or upper walls must be within 3 tiles of where Dupes stand to count toward Room eligibility.
  • Height matters for some rooms: Luxury Barracks and Private Bedroom need at least 4 tiles of ceiling height for the comfy bed to fit and the room to register.
  • Wild plants in Parks/Nature Reserves: must be untended (no farm tile / no Farmer's Touch). If a Dupe farms them, the plant is no longer "wild" and the room bonus is lost.
  • Doors: Mechanized Airlock counts as a wall tile (keeps room sealed). Manual Door also works but lets gas pass. Always use Mechanized Airlock for sealed rooms.

Phase 3 (Cycles 15–35) — Morale Upgrade

  1. Washroom — upgrade from Latrine; flush toilets + plumbed wash (+2)
  2. Great Hall — upgrade from Mess Hall once you have decor (+4 vs +3)
  3. Massage Clinic — doubles stress recovery; critical if stress is spiking

Phase 4–5 (Cycles 35–60) — Efficiency

  1. Power Plant — enables Power Control Station (+50% generator output)
  2. Greenhouse — enables Farmer's Touch (+20% crop yield)
  3. Recreation Room — gives Dupes a morale top-up between shifts

Phase 5–6 (Cycles 60+) — Luxury / Endgame

  1. Luxury Barracks or Private Bedrooms — morale +2/+3 respectively
  2. Hospital — replaces Massage Clinic when Dupes have diseases
  3. Nature Reserve — morale +6; expensive but the best morale item in the game
  4. Laboratory — +10% research speed; only worth building if you have 2+ active researchers

Sleeping Rooms

Barracks

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 4×4 to 8×5 (leave at least 2-tile ceiling height per cot)
  • Required: 1+ Cot(s)
  • Morale bonus: +1
  • Placement: upper-middle vertical zone; keep away from noisy machines (Coal Generator, Compressor — they generate stress). Build one wing per ~4 Dupes.
  • Common mistake: putting a Cot in the same room as a Mess Table. The room will be classified as Mess Hall and the Barracks bonus is lost.
  • Build tip: 4W×4H = 16 tiles. Put 2 cots side by side. Use a mechanized airlock as the door. This is the minimum viable barracks — expand width as Dupe count grows.

Luxury Barracks

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 8×5 minimum (needs 4-tile ceiling)
  • Required: 1+ Comfy Bed(s) + average room decor ≥ 20
  • Morale bonus: +2
  • Placement: same zone as Barracks but requires dedicated decor investment (Paintings, Plants, Sculptures above door). Build cycle 40+.
  • Common mistake: placing Comfy Bed in a 3-tile ceiling room — the bed is 1×2 (W×H) and needs the top cell clear; room won't register.

Private Bedroom

  • Size: 24–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 minimum, 8×5 recommended
  • Required: exactly 1 Comfy Bed + ALL background tiles must be Drywall (no exposed asteroid tile)
  • Morale bonus: +3 morale, +200% stamina recovery (Dupes recover stress during sleep far faster)
  • Placement: one room per Dupe — expensive in space. Build a row of identical 6×4 cells along a corridor. Each cell gets its own mechanized airlock.
  • Common mistake: leaving one exposed asteroid tile on the back wall — breaks the Private Bedroom requirement even if all other rules are met. Check every tile.

Sanitation Rooms

Latrine

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 4×4 to 6×4
  • Required: 1+ Outhouse(s) + 1+ Hand Sanitizer(s)
  • Morale bonus: +1
  • Placement: near living quarters; place early (Cycle 1-3). Build 1 Outhouse per 4 Dupes. Put the Hand Sanitizer between the Outhouses and the exit door.
  • Build tip: a 4×4 room fits 2 Outhouses + 1 Hand Sanitizer with room to walk. That's 16 tiles — valid.

Washroom

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 to 8×4
  • Required: 1+ Flush Toilet(s) + 1+ Sink(s) (Plumbed)
  • Morale bonus: +2
  • Placement: replaces Latrine once plumbing is live (Cycle 20-30). Flush Toilets require water input and polluted water output — place on the utility column side so pipes are short. 1 Toilet per 4 Dupes; 1 Sink at the exit.
  • Common mistake: leaving Outhouses in the same room as Flush Toilets — the room can't be both Latrine and Washroom. Remove Outhouses before adding Flush Toilets.

Dining Rooms

Mess Hall

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 to 10×4
  • Required: 1+ Mess Table(s)
  • Morale bonus: +3
  • Placement: central lower-middle zone; Dupes travel here every cycle. Keep it close to the main transit shaft to minimize travel time. Build Cycle 2-5. 1 Mess Table per 3 Dupes.
  • Build tip: a 6×4 room fits 2 Mess Tables and a walkway. That's one of the fastest +3 morale boosts in the game — higher ROI than anything else at this phase.

Great Hall

  • Size: 32–120 tiles | Practical W×H: 8×5 to 12×6
  • Required: 1+ Dining Table(s) + at least 1 decor item scoring ≥ 20 (Painting, Sculpture, etc.) + 1 Recreation Building (Juicer, Espresso Machine, Arcade Cabinet, etc.)
  • Morale bonus: +4
  • Placement: central zone. Needs minimum 32 tiles, so plan a large hall from the start rather than retrofitting. Build Cycle 30-40 when you have access to Decor Research. Keep the Recreation Building accessible to non-diners too.
  • Common mistake: using a Mess Table instead of a Dining Table — they are different buildings. Mess Table only qualifies for Mess Hall; Dining Table qualifies for Great Hall / Banquet Hall.

Banquet Hall

  • Size: 32–120 tiles | Practical W×H: 10×6 minimum
  • Required: 1+ Communal Dining Table(s) + 1 Displayed Ornament (sculptures on pedestal, not just on floor) + 1 Recreation Building
  • Morale bonus: +6
  • Placement: late-game upgrade to Great Hall. The Displayed Ornament must be on a Display Pedestal. Plan room height ≥ 5 tiles for the pedestal + sculpture stack. Build cycle 60+.

Kitchen

  • Size: 12–96 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 to 8×5
  • Required: 1 Spice Grinder + 1 Electric Grill (or Gas Range) + 1 Refrigerator
  • Effect: enables the Spice Grinder building (only works inside a Kitchen room)
  • Placement: adjacent to the Great Hall or Mess Hall; keep the Refrigerator near the food storage area. Build when you unlock Spice Grinder research (Cycle 40+). Not a morale room itself but a prerequisite for spiced food morale.

Medical Rooms

Massage Clinic

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 to 8×4
  • Required: 1+ Massage Table(s) + average room decor ≥ 20
  • Effect: doubles the stress recovery rate while a Dupe is being massaged
  • Placement: near sleeping quarters. Build Cycle 20-30 if Dupes are stress-spiking. 1 Massage Table per ~4 Dupes is sufficient — stress recovery happens during breaks, not constantly.

Hospital

  • Required: 1 Cot or Sick Bay + 1 Diagnostic Terminal or Apothecary + 1 Flush Toilet + 1 Sink
  • Effect: Dupes recover from diseases faster; enables Doctoring skill usage
  • Placement: isolated wing away from main living area — sick Dupes spread diseases. Plumbing required (needs water + polluted water pipes). Build Cycle 50+ or immediately if a disease outbreak occurs.

Production Rooms

Power Plant

  • Size: 12–96 tiles | Practical W×H: 8×5 to 14×6
  • Required: 1+ Heavy-Watt generator (Steam Turbine, Natural Gas Generator, Petroleum Generator) + 2+ any power buildings (Batteries, Transformers, Switch)
  • Effect: enables Power Control Station, which gives +50% generator output when a Power Engineer works it
  • Placement: bottom or industrial zone, far from living areas — generators produce heat and noise. The Heavi-Watt corridor runs from here outward. Mechanized Airlock doors to keep heat isolated.
  • Common mistake: using a small manual generator — it doesn't qualify as a "Heavy-Watt generator" for the Power Plant room type. Needs Natural Gas Generator or higher tier.

Greenhouse

  • Size: 12–96 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 to 10×6
  • Required: 1+ Farm Station(s)
  • Effect: enables Farmer's Touch skill (+1 harvest per plant per cycle, ~20% yield increase)
  • Placement: farming zone with farm tiles. The Farm Station must be in the same room as the farm tiles you want buffed. Keep CO2 low (farms suffer in high CO2) — vent CO2 out or use a Gas Pump.

Stable

  • Size: 12–96 tiles | Practical W×H: 8×5 to 14×6
  • Required: 1+ Grooming Station or Shearing Station (depends on creature type)
  • Effect: enables Ranching skill; critters are tamed and produce resources
  • Placement: completely isolated from main base — critters spread germs, excrete polluted materials, and make noise. Use a dedicated sub-section with its own ventilation. Hatch Stables are the most common early stable (hatches eat raw minerals and excrete coal).
  • Common mistake: mixing different creature types in one Stable. Each creature type needs its own room (e.g., Hatches in one room, Pufts in another).

6. The SPOM — Self-Powered Oxygen Machine ^ top

A sealed room using water → oxygen, that powers itself with the hydrogen byproduct. Provides oxygen for ~8 Dupes with ZERO external power.

Requires: Electrolyzer (Advanced Research / Super Computer tier) | Target cycle: ~cycle 30

Components

  • 1x Electrolyzer (water → 888 g/s Oxygen + 112 g/s Hydrogen)
  • 2x Gas Pumps (each pumps 500 g/s; two exactly match one electrolyzer)
  • 1x Gas Filter (separates H2 from O2) — or use the no-filter method
  • 1x Hydrogen Generator (burns H2 to power the whole machine)
  • Atmo Sensor(s) (keep pressure from dropping so gases don't mix)

Key Numbers

  • 1 Electrolyzer feeds ~8 Dupes and exactly supplies 2 gas pumps
  • A working SPOM nets ~600-650 kg of oxygen per cycle (often more)

Gas Separation Trick (saves a filter)

Hydrogen naturally rises above Oxygen. Place one Gas Pump HIGH (grabs H2) and one LOW (grabs O2) in the room — no Gas Filter needed. Use Atmo Sensors so pressure stays high enough to keep the two gas layers from mixing.

Cooling

The Electrolyzer and Hydrogen Generator run hot. Either build the machine out of GOLD AMALGAM (high overheat temp — usually no cooling needed), or add cooling (Wheezewort / Anti Entropy Thermo-Nullifier).

7. Room Requirements (Sizes) — for Morale Bonuses ^ top

  • MESS HALL: Low requirements; Mess Tables in an enclosed room. Best EARLY morale source.
  • BARRACKS: 12-64 tiles, cots enclosed, NO industrial machinery. Beds here restore extra stamina.
  • GREAT HALL: 32-120 tiles, ≥1 Dining Table, decor item rated 20+, NO industrial machinery. Big morale boost when eating here.
  • RECREATION ROOM: Up to 96 tiles; recreation buildings + scheduled downtime further improves morale.

8. Duplicant Skills, Leveling, Morale & Stress ^ top

Skills / Leveling

  • Dupes earn Skill Points by working; spend them in the Skill Tree
  • The tree has 12 skill groups (32 total purchasable skills)
  • Match skills to a Dupe's INTEREST: -1 effective morale cost and faster work
  • DIGGING is one of the most valuable early skills

Morale vs Stress (the make-or-break system)

  • Every skill a Dupe learns RAISES their required morale by that skill's tier
  • If morale < required morale, STRESS climbs fast until the Dupe "breaks" (stress response). Stress overload is the #1 cause of colony collapse.
  • DON'T over-train Dupes early. Save high-tier jobs until AFTER you've built morale sources.
  • Build morale BEFORE you buy expensive skills.

9. Quick-Reference Checklist ^ top

  • START: Terra + No Sweat. Pick Digger + Builder + Researcher (by interest). Avoid Anemic / Mouth Breather / Bottomless Stomach / Flatulent / Loud Sleeper / Narcolepsy.
  • C1-3: Outhouse, Wash Basin, Cots, Research Station, Manual Gen + Battery, Oxygen Diffuser. Research = Farming.
  • C3-10: Planter Boxes (Mealwood), Microbe Musher, Pitcher Pump, Mess Hall. Research = Power Regulation → Plumbing/Sanitation.
  • C10-30: Lavatory + plumbing, Water Sieve, Coal gens (remote!), Smart Battery, Barracks, Super Computer. Research = toward Electrolyzer.
  • ~C30: BUILD THE SPOM (oxygen for 8 Dupes, self-powered).
  • C30-50: Great Hall, Recreation Room, Kitchen, decor → morale.
  • C50-100: Automate power, expand farms/oxygen, start Ranching, refine metals, seal biomes with insulated tiles + airlocks.
  • ALWAYS: Manage stress > everything. Heat sources far from base. Don't breach biomes early. Build morale before training high-tier skills.

10. General How-To Tips ^ top

10.1 How to Reuse / Recycle Water ^ top

  • Water is a CLOSED LOOP. Toilets/showers output Polluted Water → pipe to Water Sieve → converts back to clean Water → drink, farm, electrolyze.
  • Build the loop: Lavatory/Shower → polluted-water riser DOWN → reservoir → Water Sieve → clean-water riser UP → back to consumers
  • Water Sieve needs SAND as filtration medium; outputs water at ~40C; does NOT remove germs or much heat
  • Kill germs with time, Chlorine gas in storage, or by cooking the food
  • Outhouses produce POLLUTED DIRT (use on Pincha Peppernuts or compost); plumbed Lavatories give Polluted Water — switch to Lavatories ASAP
  • Liquid Reservoir buffers surges so the sieve isn't starved
  • Don't dump clean water outputs and polluted inputs into the same pipe

10.2 How to Generate Electricity (Early → Late) ^ top

  • MANUAL GENERATOR (cycle 1): ~400W while a Dupe runs it; no fuel, ties up a Dupe. Starter/backup only.
  • COAL GENERATOR (early-mid): 600W, burns Coal, emits CO2 + heat. Sealed, insulated power room far from bedrooms.
  • HYDROGEN GENERATOR: 800W from H2 your SPOM makes — what makes a SPOM "self-powered."
  • NATURAL GAS GENERATOR (mid): 800W, burns Natural Gas, emits Polluted Water + CO2.
  • Backbone: COAL → HYDROGEN (SPOM) → NATURAL GAS as you find geysers
  • Always pair generators with BATTERIES; automate so they only burn fuel when batteries are low

10.3 How to Automate (Signal Wire Basics) ^ top

  • Automation wire carries a GREEN (on) / RED (off) signal, no power, no capacity limit. Separate layer from power wire.
  • SMART BATTERY: set "battery low" (~5%) to turn generators ON and "battery high" (~99%) to turn them OFF. #1 automation every base needs.
  • ATMO SENSOR → shutoff/pump: stop oxygen vents at target pressure (~1.8-2.0 kg/tile)
  • HYDRO SENSOR: gate liquid pumps so a reservoir/sieve never overfills or runs dry
  • THERMO SENSOR: switch cooling on above a target temperature
  • LOGIC GATES (AND/OR/NOT/BUFFER/FILTER): combine signals; BUFFER/FILTER add time delays to stop rapid on/off flicker
  • Start simple: smart-battery-to-generator and atmo-sensor-to-vent cover 80% of an early base

10.4 How to Plant / Farm ^ top

  • MEALWOOD in Planter Boxes is the starter crop: grows in base air, no irrigation, yields Meal Lice → cook into Liceloaf
  • Plant ~1 Mealwood per Dupe per ~1.5 cycles as a rough rule, then scale
  • Each plant has a TEMPERATURE and ATMOSPHERE range; check the plant's tooltip
  • WILD vs DOMESTIC: wild plant grows slower but needs ZERO inputs. Domestic in Planter Box/Farm Tile with fertilizer grows faster but consumes resources.
  • FARM TILES let you pipe fertilizer/water in for higher yield
  • Keep a small surplus and a REFRIGERATOR/ration box; rot wastes food
  • Don't over-plant before you can keep the room in-range (temp/pressure)

10.5 How to Breed / Ranch (Critters) ^ top

  • Build a STABLE room (Critter Drop-Off + a groomed space). HATCHES are the staple — eat raw minerals and poop COAL (sustainable fuel)
  • Keep ~6-8 critters per stable; OVERCROWDING makes them unhappy and stops reproduction
  • EGGS hatch into baby critters; excess eggs can be cooked (Omelette) or left to hatch. Incubators speed breeding.
  • SAGE HATCH eats Dirt/Polluted Dirt and makes coal; SMOOTH HATCH eats refined metal/ore for refined metal
  • Assign a Dupe the RANCHING skill so grooming is fast

10.6 How to Dig (Excavation Efficiency) ^ top

  • Prioritize a DIGGER Dupe (interest in Excavation): faster digging, cheap morale
  • Set priority 9 on urgent tiles (escape routes, the next room)
  • DON'T breach unknown biome walls early — they hide hot gases, germs, and floods
  • Watch what falls: digging from UNDER liquids/sand causes spills/cave-ins; dig from the side or top
  • Abyssalite and dense rock dig slowly — route around them early
  • Set STORAGE so haulers tidy debris automatically (loose debris lowers decor and slows pathing)

10.7 How to Store Water & Waste Water ^ top

  • Liquids pool by density in open tiles. Simple "tank" = sealed pit. Keep clean and polluted water in SEPARATE pits.
  • LIQUID RESERVOIR (5,000 kg) = compact buffer with Hydro Sensor to stop overfill
  • Store WASTE water SEALED and away from breathing areas — it off-gasses Polluted Oxygen
  • Keep a few cycles of clean-water RESERVE so a sieve hiccup doesn't dry out your farms
  • Insulate reservoirs/pits that hold temperature-critical liquid with Insulated Tiles

10.8 How to Do Storage for Each Facility ^ top

  • GENERAL: storage = SHORT HAULS. Put each material near where it's consumed; use filter to whitelist only what belongs there.
  • POWER ROOM: Storage Bin set to COAL right beside generators → max uptime, minimal fetch
  • KITCHEN / FOOD HALL: REFRIGERATOR (or Ration Box early) next to cook and tables; separate bin for raw ingredients. Sealed sterile air slows spoilage.
  • FARM: small bin for seeds/fertilizer near planters; bin for harvested crop feeding into kitchen
  • RANCH: feeder bin for critters' food inside stable; egg/meat bin near drop-off
  • INDUSTRY / REFINERY: ore bins beside refinery; output (refined metal) bin nearby. Keep far from living base.
  • MATERIAL/GENERAL STORAGE: central-lower storage room for raw rock/ore; set filters per bin so it's sorted
  • Mark loose debris to Sweep, set storage priority so idle Dupes tidy automatically
  • Storing solids in a vacuum or cold room stops food rot and heat leaking (late-game trick)

10.9 Misc Quick Tips ^ top

  • OXYGEN: gases stratify by weight — O2 sinks, CO2 sinks below it, Hydrogen/Polluted O2 rise. Source oxygen ABOVE living space, pump CO2 from the floor, vent H2 high.
  • HEAT is the silent killer: it never disappears, only moves. Keep generators/refineries behind Insulated Tiles. Use Wheezeworts (free cooling) early.
  • DECOR & MORALE: scatter decor items, keep floors clear of debris, don't over-train Dupes.
  • GERMS: Food Poisoning spreads via dirty hands and contaminated water/food — a Wash Basin/Sink on toilet exits is the cheapest defense. Slimelung lives in polluted oxygen/slime biomes; seal those off.
  • SCHEDULE: give Dupes a proper sleep + downtime block; tweak the colony schedule so work, eat, recreation, and sleep don't collide into bottlenecks.

11.2 Power Progression by Game Phase ^ top

Phase 1 (Cycles 0–10): Manual + Coal starter

  • Manual Generator (1) to power the Research Station and first Oxygen Diffusers.
  • Transition to Coal Generator as soon as you have coal and have researched Combustion.
  • Target: 1 Coal Generator (~600 W) powering research, 2 diffusers, and a battery buffer.

Phase 2 (Cycles 10–30): Coal bank + Smart Battery

  • Add Coal Generators as Dupe count and building count grow. Budget ~120–200 W per Dupe for a comfortable base.
  • Install a Smart Battery and automation immediately when the first coal gen goes in (see 11.7).
  • Target: 2–4 Coal Generators with a Smart Battery, feeding a Large Transformer per floor.

Phase 3 (~Cycle 30): The SPOM comes online

  • The SPOM's Hydrogen Generator generates 800 W and consumes all its own hydrogen — net zero cost from the coal grid.
  • The SPOM's oxygen production replaces all Algae/Diffuser oxygen — you can decommission those and reclaim the power they used.

Phase 4 (Cycles 40–70): Natural Gas

  • Tap a Natural Gas Geyser → pipe gas to a Natural Gas Generator (800 W) for free renewable power.
  • One average Natural Gas Geyser (~100–200 g/s average) can power 1–2 generators sustainably. Use a Gas Reservoir as a buffer.

Phase 5 (Cycles 70+): Petroleum / Steam Turbines

  • Petroleum Generator (2,000 W) is the highest single-building output; requires Oil Refinery chain.
  • Steam Turbines pair well with volcanic geysers; they output cooled water usable in cooling loops.
  • Combine: Coal → Hydrogen (SPOM) → Natural Gas → Petroleum as each tier comes online.

11.3 Grid Architecture ^ top

ONI circuits overload when total draw exceeds wire capacity. The solution is to isolate the generator backbone from consumer circuits using Power Transformers.

The two-tier grid:

TIER 1 — BACKBONE (high-capacity wire)
  Generators + Batteries
        |
  [Heavi-Watt Wire or Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire]
        |
  [Power Transformer per zone/floor]
        |
TIER 2 — CONSUMER CIRCUIT (cheap wire)
  Standard Wire (1,000 W) or Conductive Wire (2,000 W)
        |
  Individual buildings on that floor

Grid design rules:

  • Each Power Transformer creates a fully independent consumer circuit on its output side. An overload on one circuit does not trip any other.
  • Add up the wattage of all buildings on a floor. If it exceeds 1,000 W, use Conductive Wire or add a second transformer.
  • Generators and Batteries connect on the BACKBONE side of the transformer (input). Rooms connect on the OUTPUT side. Never mix the two sides.
  • Use the Power Overlay (press P) — white = fine, yellow = near limit, red = overloaded.
  • Wire Bridges break BEFORE wires when overloaded — place a bridge at an easy-to-reach spot on each circuit as a sacrificial fuse.

11.5 Heavi-Watt Wire Rules ^ top

  • Heavi-Watt Wire CANNOT be embedded inside a solid tile (unlike normal wire). It must sit in an open air cell.
  • It carries a −25 decor penalty over a 6-tile radius. Any Dupe who can see or path within 6 tiles will lose decor, causing stress. Keep it completely hidden. The total affected zone spans 13 tiles wide (6 tiles left + 1 wire tile + 6 tiles right) — see diagram below.
  • Run it through a dedicated 1-tile-wide sealed corridor: wall tile on each side, Heavi-Watt wire in the empty cell between. No Dupe access. No windows.

11.7 Power Automation ^ top

The essential automation: Smart Battery → Generators

[Smart Battery]
  LOW threshold (~5%)  → sends GREEN → all Generators turn ON
  HIGH threshold (~99%) → sends RED   → all Generators turn OFF
  • Wire: Smart Battery automation output → each Generator's automation input port.
  • Set LOW at 5% and HIGH at 99% to avoid rapid on/off flicker (generators burn fuel even during the startup spin-up).
  • Add a BUFFER Gate on the signal if generators still flicker — it adds a time delay (set to 5–10 seconds) before the signal passes, preventing rapid on/off cycles.
  • With this setup, coal generators only run when needed — coal consumption drops dramatically vs. running them constantly.

Multi-generator priority: stagger your generators

  • Wire Generator 1 to fire at 20% battery, Generator 2 at 10%, Generator 3 at 5%.
  • Use multiple Smart Batteries with different thresholds, each controlling one generator.
  • Result: the cheapest/dirtiest generator (coal) fires last; the cleanest/freest (hydrogen) fires first.

Automation for specific generators:

  • Hydrogen Generator (SPOM): best controlled by a separate Smart Battery inside the SPOM room so it only fires when the SPOM's own battery is low. Do not connect it to the main battery bank directly or it will fire constantly and waste hydrogen.
  • Natural Gas Generator: add a Gas Reservoir on the supply line with a Hydro Sensor. Wire sensor to generator automation: if gas pressure drops below a safe threshold, shut the generator off to avoid running empty and damaging the pipe.
  • Steam Turbine: controlled by a Thermo Sensor on the steam input. Turbine runs when steam temperature is above 125°C; shuts off when steam is exhausted.

11.8 Common Power Mistakes ^ top

  • Running generators without a Smart Battery: generators run 24/7 burning all fuel even when no power is needed. Smart Battery + automation cuts fuel use by 50–80%.
  • Connecting a Battery on the consumer side of a Transformer: the battery only buffers that circuit's output cap (1,000–4,000 W), not the whole base. Put all main batteries on the backbone side.
  • Routing Heavi-Watt wire near living areas: the −25 decor hits every room within 6 tiles, causing stress and morale collapse. It must be in a sealed, hidden corridor.
  • Overloading one circuit: all buildings on a Standard Wire circuit share 1,000 W. A Coal Generator (600 W) + two Oxygen Diffusers (120 W each) + Research Station (100 W) = 940 W — nearly at the limit. Add one more building and it trips. Use the Power Overlay constantly.
  • Building a Power Plant room with Coal Generators: coal gens do not qualify as "Heavy-Watt generators" for the room type. You need a Natural Gas Gen, Petroleum Gen, or Steam Turbine.
  • Placing Coal Generators near bedrooms: they emit 4 kDTU/s of heat and produce CO2. They will heat your base and poison your sleeping area. Always seal them in an insulated room at the bottom or far side of the base.
  • Forgetting that Heavi-Watt Wire needs a Joint Plate to cross a wall tile: you cannot simply run it through a wall like normal wire. If you skip the Joint Plate, the wire stops at the wall and your backbone is broken.
  • Not sealing the SPOM before the Hydrogen Generator fires: if the SPOM room is open to the base, hydrogen will escape into living areas. Seal the SPOM room first, then turn it on.

12.5 Storage & Reservoirs ^ top

  • A Liquid Reservoir holds 5,000 kg and acts as a buffer. Set an automation sensor (Reservoir Sensor output) to stop the pump when full.
  • For long-term bulk storage, flood a sealed room and pump in/out. A 5×5 tile room holds ~25,000 kg of water. Label it with a Sign.
  • Use a Hydro Sensor on a flooded chamber to automate fill/drain based on liquid height.
  • Insulate water storage rooms with Insulation tiles if temperature is critical (e.g., storing 20°C water near hot machinery).

12.6 Tapping Geysers ^ top

Geysers erupt in cycles: active period (high flow) and dormant period (no flow). Always build a buffer reservoir to cover dormant gaps.

  • Analyze first: use the Geyser Analyzer (requires a Duplicant with Science). Read the average kg/s output, eruption rate, dormancy rate, and temperature.
  • Water Geyser tap: dig to the geyser tile, build a sealed room around it, place a Liquid Pump in the room, pipe output to a reservoir. Add a Hydro Sensor on the reservoir to stop the pump when full (pump idles; no power wasted).
  • Cooling hot water: route through a Thermo Aquatuner (use polluted water or oil as coolant) to bring temperature down before use. Target <70°C for safe piping and plant irrigation.
  • Cool Steam Vent: vent outputs steam into a sealed steam chamber; place a Steam Turbine above to condense it into water and generate power simultaneously.

12.7 Temperature & Heat in Liquids ^ top

  • Liquids transfer heat to tiles and buildings they touch. Hot water from a Water Geyser (95°C) will heat your pipes and eventually your base if not cooled.
  • Thermo Aquatuner: 1,200 W; drops liquid temperature by exactly 14°C per pass; the extracted heat goes to the surrounding environment. Use it in an insulated room filled with a high-thermal-mass liquid (Petroleum or Brine) to prevent runaway heat build-up.
  • Insulated Pipe (made of Igneous Rock, Granite, or better: Abyssalite) minimizes heat exchange while liquid is in transit. Use it everywhere you move hot or cold liquids through inhabited areas.
  • Water freezes at 0°C; if you are running water through a cold biome, use insulated pipes or it will freeze and burst the pipe.
  • Water boils at 100°C in standard pressure environments. Keep hot-water loops below 95°C to avoid phase changes inside pipes.

12.8 Common Liquid Mistakes ^ top

  • No water loop / no Water Sieve: you run out of sand and flush polluted water away, losing hundreds of kg per cycle. Build the Lavatory→Sieve→Reservoir loop by Cycle 5.
  • Liquid Pump not submerged: a Liquid Pump placed in a dry tile does nothing. It needs at least 1 tile of liquid above it to activate (mini pump = 1 tile, standard pump = 2 tiles).
  • Running out of sand for Water Sieve: the Sieve eats 1 kg sand per kg water. A 5 dupes base uses ~75 kg/cycle. Stock >2,000 kg before tapping a geyser. Regolith from the surface can be crushed into Sand.
  • Hot water in uninsulated pipes through cold biome: water freezes mid-pipe and the pipe breaks, flooding the cold biome with water that then re-freezes as tiles. Always use Insulated Pipe in cold biomes.
  • Polluted water pool open to air above 25°C: emits Polluted Oxygen (PO2), contaminating your base atmosphere. Seal all polluted water storage or keep it cold.
  • Flooded reservoirs without a vent: if a liquid reservoir pipe has no gas vent/pocket, pressure builds and the pump can stall. Always leave a small gas pocket above flooded storage rooms.
  • Not accounting for geyser dormancy: a geyser that averages 2 kg/s still has periods of zero output. Without a buffer reservoir, your base runs dry during dormancy. Build a buffer sized for the longest dormancy period.

13.4 CO2 Management ^ top

CO2 sinks to the floor. If unmanaged, it builds up and suffocates dupes working at floor level (especially in Mining, Farming, and Generator rooms).

Solutions (pick one or combine):

  • CO2 Pocket at bottom of base: dig a sealed pit 2–4 tiles deep at the base of your main shaft. CO2 sinks into it naturally. No power needed. Just don't send dupes there.
  • Carbon Skimmer: 120 W; requires 1 kg/s Polluted Water; absorbs CO2 from the air in its tile. Outputs Polluted Water. Connect to your polluted water network. Place at floor level where CO2 pools.
  • Algae Distiller: converts CO2 + Water into Algae + Polluted Water. Useful early when you have excess algae tanks. 80 W.
  • Vent to space: mid-to-late game option. Pump CO2 to the surface and vent. Requires a sealed corridor with a Gas Pump at the bottom and a vent at the top of the map.

CO2 room rule: always place a Gas Pump + pipe in any generator room and route CO2 to a dedicated pit. Coal generators, Petroleum generators, and Natural Gas generators all produce CO2.

13.5 Oxygen Distribution ^ top

Oxygen needs to reach every room dupes inhabit. It can travel by pipe or by natural diffusion through open tiles.

  • Oxygen Diffuser (early): 120 W; outputs 41.67 g/s of O2; consumes Algae (100 g/s). Algae is finite — it's a temporary solution only.
  • SPOM (Cycle 25–35 target): Electrolyzer-based O2 production. Self-powered via Hydrogen Generator. Outputs ~200 g/s net O2. Replace all Diffusers.
  • Oxygen distribution: vent O2 at mid-height in each major room. O2 will spread by diffusion into adjacent tiles. For large bases (more than 5 floors), pipe O2 to a Gas Vent on each floor rather than relying on diffusion from one point.
  • Overpressure: a Gas Vent stops outputting when tile pressure >1.8 kg. Use High-Pressure Gas Vents only in sealed rooms designed for it; normal vents are fine for open living areas.

13.6 Gas Filters & Reservoirs ^ top

  • Gas Filter: critical in SPOMs to separate H2 from O2. Place on the Electrolyzer output pipe. Set filter target to Hydrogen. H2 exits the secondary port; O2 (and everything else) passes straight through.
  • Gas Reservoir: buffer between a geyser and its consumer (e.g., Natural Gas Geyser → Reservoir → Natural Gas Generator). Without a reservoir, the generator chokes during dormancy. Size the reservoir to cover the dormancy period.
  • Natural Gas Geyser tapping: dig sealed room, Gas Pump at the top of the pocket (natural gas rises), pipe to reservoir. A 150 kg reservoir covers roughly 5 minutes of generator runtime at full output.

13.7 Special Gases ^ top

Chlorine

  • Found in Chlorine biomes. Extremely toxic to dupes. Never allow it into inhabited areas.
  • Use: seal a small room with Chlorine. Place a Seed Safe or equipment inside. Chlorine kills all germs passively. Excellent for sanitizing seeds before Cycle 30 (before you have Bleach Stone sanitation).
  • Chlorine Geyser: rare but provides infinite supply. Pump output into a sealed storage chamber. Use it for germ control throughout the game.

Natural Gas

  • Produced by Natural Gas Geysers and in tiny amounts by Lavatories.
  • Rises (lower density than O2). Seal the top of your Natural Gas Generator room so it accumulates there naturally, reducing pumping needs.
  • One Natural Gas Geyser typically averages ~100–200 g/s. A Natural Gas Generator needs 90 g/s. Most single geysers can sustain one generator perpetually.

Steam

  • Only useful when directed into a Steam Turbine. Otherwise it is waste heat in gas form.
  • Steam Turbines require steam at ≥125°C to run; output cooled water at ~95°C.
  • Build Steam Turbines above active geysers or above your hot-side heat sink in a cooling loop.

13.8 Common Gas Mistakes ^ top

  • CO2 pooling at floor level unnoticed: dupes randomly pass out and are displayed as "suffocating." Check the Gas Overlay; CO2 at floor level is almost always the cause. Add a Carbon Skimmer or CO2 pit immediately.
  • Placing a Gas Pump in the wrong position: the Gas Pump pumps the gas at the tile directly above its intake. Put the pump where the target gas is densest (e.g., for CO2 at the floor, put the pump at floor level; for H2 at the ceiling, put the pump near the ceiling).
  • SPOM not sealed before startup: if the SPOM room is open to the base, hydrogen drifts into living areas. Seal all walls with Insulated Tiles or regular tiles before activating the Electrolyzer.
  • Gas Vent overpressure: the vent stops venting when room pressure exceeds 1.8 kg/tile. If your vent shows an overpressure warning, the room is full. Either expand the room or reduce vent output.
  • No buffer reservoir on Natural Gas line: the geyser erupts and the generator gets a surge, then nothing during dormancy. The generator idles burning no fuel during dormancy but loses power output — add a reservoir to smooth the supply.
  • Letting Chlorine escape: even one tile of Chlorine in a dupe path causes incapacitation. Seal Chlorine rooms with Airflow Tiles only where you want controlled gas flow; use solid tiles everywhere else.

14.7 Food Germs & Spoilage ^ top

  • Food Poisoning germs: if dupes work in a contaminated area (polluted water puddles, bathrooms) without washing hands, they carry germs to food. This causes Food Poisoning morale debuff and vomiting, which contaminates more surfaces.
  • Sink placement: place a Sink (Hand Sanitizer or Wash Basin) outside every Lavatory and every Outhouse. Dupes wash hands after use before touching food storage.
  • Food storage: use a Ration Box (stores 20 kg food, no power, no germ protection) or a Refrigerator (requires 80 W, keeps food cold, slows germ growth significantly). Refrigerator is the priority upgrade around Cycle 20.
  • Spoilage: food does not technically expire in the base game (unlike some DLC settings), but food exposed to germs will become contaminated and cause illness. Keep food storage away from polluted water and outhouses.
  • Chlorine germ kill: a sealed Chlorine room can sanitize any food placed inside it for a few cycles. Useful for clearing contaminated Bristle Berries.

14.9 Common Farming Mistakes ^ top

  • Too few Mealwood plants early: the target is 2 Mealwood per dupe (each produces 200 kcal/cycle). For 5 dupes that's 10 plants. Underbuilding here causes food crises at Cycle 10–15 when dupes eat stored reserves faster than plants can grow.
  • Mealwood overheating: Mealwood dies above 30°C. Early bases often run hot due to machines. Place Mealwood farms away from generators and near cold biome tiles. If the farm heats up, the entire food supply can die simultaneously.
  • Using Planter Boxes and expecting a Farm room bonus: Planter Boxes do not count for the Farm room type. Always switch to Farm Tiles before building your mid-game greenhouse.
  • No hand-washing station: the most common cause of Food Poisoning outbreaks. Place a Sink at every Lavatory exit. This one building can prevent colony-wide illness crises.
  • Irrigating with unfiltered polluted water: Pincha Pepperplant can use polluted water, but other plants cannot. Using polluted water on regular crops kills them and can spread germs. Connect the correct water type to each crop type.
  • Not planning for crop transition: switching from Mealwood to Bristle Blossom is a vulnerable period — Mealwood is decommissioned, Bristle Blossom not yet mature. Always keep the old farm running until the new one produces its first harvest.
  • Ignoring food morale until it causes a stress cascade: Mush Bars (Quality 0) give a −1 morale penalty. If dupes are near the stress threshold, a bad food cycle can trigger vomiting, which triggers more contamination, which triggers more illness. Don't let food quality stagnate past Cycle 20.

15. SPOM — Self-Powered Oxygen Machine (ONI)

15.1 What is a SPOM? ^ top

A SPOM (Self-Powered Oxygen Machine) is a sealed room containing an Electrolyzer and a Hydrogen Generator wired together so the hydrogen produced by the Electrolyzer fuels the generator, and the generator powers the Electrolyzer. The net result: the SPOM runs indefinitely on water alone, producing oxygen as a free byproduct and exporting surplus electricity to the base grid.

A working SPOM completely replaces Oxygen Diffusers and Algae Terrariums by around Cycle 25–35. It is the single most important mid-game infrastructure upgrade.

  • The Electrolyzer outputs gas and instantly pressurizes the room. The room must have enough volume to not immediately overpressure and stall the Electrolyzer. A minimum of 20 tiles of free gas space is recommended.
  • Use Insulated Tiles on all walls and floor to contain heat. The Electrolyzer generates significant heat (4 kDTU/s at output temperature 70°C). Without insulation, this heat leaks into adjacent rooms.

15.6 Water Supply ^ top

The SPOM consumes exactly 1 kg/s of water per Electrolyzer. Securing this supply is the main prerequisite before building the SPOM.

  • Requirement: 1,000 g/s (1 kg/s) per Electrolyzer, indefinitely. This is a permanent, constant drain on your water supply.
  • Sources: a single Water Geyser averaging ≥1 kg/s (most do), or a Cool Steam Vent condensed with a Steam Turbine, or accumulated ice melt from the starting biome.
  • Lavatory waste loop does NOT produce enough water for the SPOM — the loop actually yields more water than it consumes, but that surplus (~6.7 kg/cycle per dupe) is too small to feed an Electrolyzer continuously.
  • Temperature: the Electrolyzer accepts water at any temperature. However, colder water (e.g., 20°C vs 95°C from a geyser) means the SPOM room heats up less quickly. Pre-cool water with a Thermo Aquatuner before feeding the SPOM if heat is a concern.
  • Water pipe into SPOM: a single Liquid Valve set to 1 kg/s is sufficient. Use a Liquid Shutoff on an Automation signal tied to the SPOM's Smart Battery or a Water Sensor to stop water input if the Electrolyzer overpressures the room (pressure >2 kg/tile stalls the Electrolyzer).

15.7 Power Wiring ^ top

The SPOM's Hydrogen Generator must power the Electrolyzer. There are two wiring approaches:

Option A — Internal self-contained circuit (recommended):

  • Connect the Hydrogen Generator's power output to the Electrolyzer, Gas Pump, and Gas Filter on an internal wire loop inside the SPOM room.
  • Add a Smart Battery to this internal circuit. The Smart Battery controls when the Hydrogen Generator runs.
  • Connect the Smart Battery's output wire through a Transformer to the main Heavi-Watt backbone (external grid). The Transformer exports surplus power to the base.
  • Benefit: the SPOM's internal circuit is independent. Even if the external grid fails, the SPOM keeps running and producing O2.

Option B — Shared grid:

  • Wire the Hydrogen Generator directly into the base's power backbone via a Transformer, and pull power from the backbone to feed the Electrolyzer.
  • Simpler to build but creates a dependency: if the base grid goes down, the Electrolyzer loses power and O2 production stops.
  • Not recommended for colonies >5 dupes.
  Recommended wiring (Option A):

  [H2 Generator] → internal wire → [Smart Battery]
       |                                        |
  [Electrolyzer]  [Gas Pump]  [Gas Filter]  [Transformer] → Heavi-Watt backbone
       (all draw from internal wire)              (exports surplus)

15.8 Heat Management ^ top

The Electrolyzer is a significant heat source. Ignoring heat causes the SPOM room to eventually overheat, which in turn overheats the oxygen it produces, which heats the base.

Electrolyzer4 kDTU/s at 70°C output gasMain heat source in the SPOM room. Output gas temperature ≈ 70°C regardless of input water temperature.
Hydrogen Generator~4 kDTU/sSignificant. Place on a high thermal mass material (e.g., Granite tile) to absorb spikes.
Gas Pump~1 kDTU/sMinor contributor.

Heat solutions (in order of complexity):

  • Insulated walls: at minimum, surround the SPOM room with Insulated Tiles. This slows heat leakage to adjacent rooms. Does not eliminate heat inside the SPOM room, but buys time.
  • High thermal mass floor: build the SPOM floor from Granite or Igneous Rock. These materials absorb more heat per degree, flattening temperature spikes.
  • Cold water input: feed the Electrolyzer with 20°C water instead of 95°C geyser water. The difference matters — the Electrolyzer always outputs gas at 70°C, but cold water absorbs some heat as it heats up before splitting, slightly reducing net room heat.
  • AETN (if available): if the map has an Anti-Entropy Thermo-Nullifier nearby (requires Hydrogen atmosphere), connect it to the SPOM room's H2 pocket. It passively absorbs ~80 kDTU/s — completely neutralizing all SPOM heat with no power cost.
  • Thermo Aquatuner loop (active cooling): run a polluted water or petroleum loop through a Thermo Aquatuner inside the SPOM room (or an adjacent insulated hot-side room). Actively chills the SPOM to any target temperature. 1,200 W power cost but completely solves heat.

Without any cooling: the SPOM room temperature will rise over time. At ~70°C room air, the Electrolyzer and Hydrogen Generator continue to run but the O2 output gas is hot. Hot oxygen reaching Farm Tiles will kill temperature-sensitive plants. Hot O2 in living areas will slowly heat the base. Address heat by Cycle 50 at the latest.

15.9 Startup Procedure ^ top

Order of operations matters. Doing this wrong leads to hydrogen escaping into the base or the Electrolyzer stalling immediately.

  1. Build the room first. Place all Insulated Tiles on walls, ceiling, and floor. Verify the room is completely sealed — no gaps. Use the Gas Overlay to confirm no existing gas can flow in or out.
  2. Build all internal buildings (Electrolyzer, Hydrogen Generator, Gas Pump, Gas Filter, Smart Battery) and all pipes/wires inside the sealed room.
  3. Connect water supply pipe. Run the liquid pipe in through a wall (pipes pass through tiles without breaking the gas seal). Add a Liquid Shutoff on the external side so you can cut water if needed.
  4. Connect internal power circuit. Wire Hydrogen Generator → Smart Battery → Electrolyzer + Gas Pump + Gas Filter. Add Transformer to export surplus to backbone. Do NOT power the Electrolyzer from the external grid yet.
  5. Pre-fill the Smart Battery. Temporarily connect one external power source (a Coal Generator or the base grid) to the internal circuit to charge the Smart Battery to 100%.
  6. Open the water valve. Water flows in; Electrolyzer starts splitting it. H2 and O2 begin pressurizing the sealed room.
  7. Wait for H2 pressure to build. The Gas Pump will start moving gas through the Gas Filter. H2 reaches the Hydrogen Generator via its input pipe.
  8. Hydrogen Generator fires. Once the H2 pipe has gas, the generator starts (if the Smart Battery is below 100%). The generator now powers the Electrolyzer. External power can be disconnected.
  9. Verify O2 is venting to base. Check the Gas Overlay. O2 should be flowing through the Gas Vent on each floor. Confirm no H2 is reaching living areas.

15.10 Automation & Sensors ^ top

Smart Battery (primary control):

  • Set LOW threshold: 10%. Generator fires when battery drops below 10%.
  • Set HIGH threshold: 100%. Generator stops when battery is full.
  • With this setup, the generator only runs when needed — H2 is not wasted burning continuously.

Gas Pressure Sensor on H2 pipe (optional):

  • Place a Gas Pressure Sensor on the H2 pipe between the Gas Filter and the Hydrogen Generator.
  • Wire: if pressure <100 g, signal a Gas Shutoff to close the H2 line to the generator (prevents generator from running on insufficient fuel and stalling).

Liquid Sensor on water input (optional but recommended):

  • If the room pressure rises above 2 kg/tile (Electrolyzer overpressure), the Electrolyzer stops consuming water. Water continues to flood the pipes and reservoir wastes pump power.
  • Add a Gas Pressure Sensor in the SPOM room. Wire: if room pressure >1.8 kg, send signal to Liquid Shutoff to stop water input.
  • This prevents the SPOM from flooding with water if overpressure is sustained for long periods (which can happen if O2 vents are blocked downstream).

O2 flow automation:

  • If O2 production outpaces base demand (well-sealed base with low consumption), the O2 vents will hit overpressure and back up. Add a Gas Shutoff on the O2 distribution pipe, controlled by a Gas Pressure Sensor at the SPOM room: if room pressure >1.8 kg, close the water shutoff (reduce input) rather than the O2 vent.

15.11 Common SPOM Mistakes ^ top

  • Not sealing the room before activation: the most common disaster. If the SPOM room has any open tile, H2 drifts into the base atmosphere. H2 in dupes' living area is harmless to breathe but displaces O2, and in later game scenarios can ignite near heat sources (rare in base game but possible). Seal first, always.
  • Skipping the Gas Filter: without a filter, O2 and H2 mix in the same output pipe. The Hydrogen Generator receives a gas mix it cannot efficiently burn (it burns only pure H2). Output drops dramatically and the SPOM stops being self-powered.
  • Using a large Gas Pump instead of a small Gas Pump: the large Gas Pump draws 240 W vs. 60 W for the small pump. That extra 180 W consumes ~22% of the generator's output for no benefit. Always use the Mini Gas Pump inside the SPOM.
  • Connecting the Electrolyzer to the base grid directly: if the external grid overloads and trips, the Electrolyzer loses power and O2 production halts. Use an internal Smart Battery circuit (Option A in Section 15.7) so the SPOM is grid-independent.
  • Forgetting that Electrolyzer stalls above 2 kg/tile pressure: if the SPOM room fills with gas faster than the pump can move it, pressure exceeds 2 kg/tile and the Electrolyzer shuts off. Ensure the Gas Pump is actively running. Overpressure is the most common cause of a "SPOM that suddenly stopped working."
  • No water buffer reservoir: if the water source (geyser) goes dormant, water input stops and the SPOM runs the remaining pipe water dry within minutes. Add a Liquid Reservoir (5,000 kg) on the input line to buffer through dormancy periods.
  • Building the SPOM too close to living areas: Hydrogen Generators emit 4 kDTU/s of heat and have a −10 decor penalty. Insulated walls prevent heat leakage but decor still radiates through walls. Place the SPOM in a remote area, ideally at the top of the base where H2 naturally concentrates and where the heat pocket is isolated.
  • Trying to run the SPOM without researching both Improved Plumbing and Renewable Energy: the Electrolyzer is unlocked by Improved Plumbing; the Hydrogen Generator by Renewable Energy. Both must be researched before you can build a SPOM. Plan your research path accordingly — do not delay Renewable Energy past Cycle 20.
