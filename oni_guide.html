OXYGEN NOT INCLUDED — BASE GAME (VANILLA) SURVIVAL & BUILD GUIDE

Focus: Main game only (NOT the Spaced Out! DLC).

NOTE ON "LEVELS": ONI has no XP levels. Progress is measured by CYCLES (in-game day counter) and RESEARCH TIERS (tech tree). "Which level to build" = which research tier unlocks it.

HOW THIS GUIDE IS ORGANIZED: Sections 1–3 are the fast path (setup → research → what to build, cycle by cycle). Section 4 covers spatial layout. Sections 5–12 are the per-system deep references (Rooms, Power, Water, Gas, Farming, Ranching, Automation, SPOM) — each topic lives in exactly one place; the timeline and layout sections cross-reference them.

1. Quick Start ^ top

1.1 Game Setup & Starting Duplicants

Recommended for a first/safe playthrough

  • Asteroid: TERRA (standard balanced starting world)
  • Mode: "No Sweat" (stress builds slower, more forgiving)
  • You start with 3 Duplicants.

Choosing your 3 starting Duplicants

You do NOT pick a single named hero — you pick 3 randomly-generated Duplicants and can re-roll them. Two proven openings:

  • Option A (recommended): 1 Digger + 1 Builder + 1 Researcher
  • Option B (faster start): 2 Diggers + 1 Researcher

Pick by INTEREST — an interest makes matching skills effectively free in morale and speeds the work:

  • One Dupe interested in DIGGING (Excavation) → gathers all your materials
  • One Dupe interested in RESEARCH (Science) → unlocks the tech tree fast
  • One Dupe interested in BUILDING or FARMING → construction / food

A Dupe with an interest gets -1 effective morale cost on those skills (Tier 1 skills become basically free) AND completes that work type faster, with -1 stress. (Morale/stress mechanics: see Section 13.)

1.2 Negative Traits to Avoid

Avoid at all costs (early-game colony killers)

  • Anemic — very low Athletics; painfully slow movement
  • Mouth Breather — consumes far more oxygen
  • Bottomless Stomach — eats ~50% more food (1,500 kcal/day vs 1,000)
  • Flatulent — randomly emits Natural Gas into your base
  • Loud Sleeper — wakes nearby Dupes, wrecking sleep/morale
  • Narcolepsy — falls asleep anywhere, mid-task

Good/harmless traits to keep: high base Athletics/Strength, "interests" that match your needed jobs, traits with downsides you can ignore early.

1.3 One-Page Checklist

The three survival problems to solve first: TOILETS, FOOD, OXYGEN. Full detail in Section 3.

  • START: Terra + No Sweat. Pick Digger + Builder + Researcher (by interest). Avoid Anemic / Mouth Breather / Bottomless Stomach / Flatulent / Loud Sleeper / Narcolepsy.
  • C1-3: Outhouse, Wash Basin, Cots, Research Station, Manual Gen + Battery, Oxygen Diffuser. Research = Farming.
  • C3-10: Planter Boxes (Mealwood), Microbe Musher, Pitcher Pump, Mess Hall. Research = Power Regulation → Plumbing/Sanitation.
  • C10-30: Lavatory + plumbing, Water Sieve, Coal gens (remote!), Smart Battery, Barracks, Super Computer. Research = toward Electrolyzer.
  • ~C30: BUILD THE SPOM (oxygen for 8 Dupes, self-powered — see Section 12).
  • C30-50: Great Hall, Recreation Room, Kitchen, decor → morale.
  • C50-100: Automate power, expand farms/oxygen, start Ranching, refine metals, seal biomes with insulated tiles + airlocks.
  • ALWAYS: Manage stress > everything. Heat sources far from base. Don't breach biomes early. Build morale before training high-tier skills.

2. Research / Tech Tree ^ top

2.1 Priority Overview

Build a Research Station EARLY (cycle 1-2). Research ONE branch at a time — half-researched branches give you nothing. Top priority first:

  1. FOOD branch first — "Farming" → unlocks Planter Box (grow Mealwood) and Liceloaf. Starting rations run out ~cycle 6.
  2. POWER / "Power Regulation" — extends generator/wire capacity; needed before Coal generators scale.
  3. LIQUID & GAS PLUMBING / "Plumbing" + "Sanitation" — Pipes, Gas/Liquid Pumps, Lavatory, Water Sieve → closes water and toilet loops.
  4. ADVANCED RESEARCH (Super Computer) — unlocks Electrolyzer (→ SPOM), Algae/oxygen tech, automation, ranching, refinement.
  5. RANCHING (mid-game) — more efficient than pure farming; gives derived products. Pursue once survival basics are stable.
  6. AUTOMATION / REFINEMENT — automation wires, Metal Refinery, temperature management. Mid/late game.

General rule: Food + simple Power first, then Plumbing/Gas to move fluids, then Advanced systems as the colony grows.

2.2 Complete Route (Step by Step)

Build a RESEARCH STATION on cycle 1. Around cycle 10-15 build a SUPER COMPUTER to unlock advanced (green) techs.

P0-P1 (Cycles 0-3) — Survival

  1. BASIC FARMING — Planter Box, Microbe Musher. THE #1 first pick — starting rations run out ~cycle 6.
  2. POWER REGULATION — Battery, Power Transformer. Store manual generator's power and prep the backbone+transformer grid.

P2 (Cycles 3-10) — Food + Move Fluids

  1. FARM STATION / AGRICULTURE — Farm Tile + fertilization.
  2. VENTILATION — Gas Pump, Gas Pipe, Gas Vent, Deodorizer/Air Filter.
  3. PLUMBING — Liquid Pump, Liquid Pipe, Liquid Vent, Bottle Emptier.
  4. SANITATION — Lavatory, Sink, Water Sieve. Closes the water + toilet loop.

P3 (Cycles 10-30) — Close Loops, Scale Power, Prep the SPOM

(Build the SUPER COMPUTER early in this phase.)

  1. COMBUSTION — Coal Generator. First scalable power; pair with batteries.
  2. ADVANCED POWER REGULATION — Smart Battery, Conductive Wire, Heavi-Watt Wire, Power Shutoff.
  3. LIQUID/IMPROVED PLUMBING — Liquid Reservoir, valves, liquid sensors. (Also unlocks the Electrolyzer.)
  4. IMPROVED VENTILATION — Gas Filter, Gas Reservoir, Insulated Gas Pipe, gas sensors.
  5. RENEWABLE ENERGY — Hydrogen Generator. Research this BEFORE building the SPOM.

P4 (~Cycle 30) — Build the SPOM

No new tech strictly required if the above are done. You now have Electrolyzer + Gas Pumps + Gas Filter + Hydrogen Generator + Insulated Gas Pipe. Build the SPOM (Section 12).

P5 (Cycles 30-50) — Comfort / Morale

  1. INTERIOR DECOR — Decor items, art, flower pots. Hit Great Hall's decor 20+ requirement.
  2. FOOD QUALITY / REJUVENATION — Refrigerator, Electric Grill / Gas Range.
  3. PLUMBING COMFORT — Shower + bathroom fittings.
  4. MEDICINE (I) — Apothecary / basic meds.

P6 (Cycles 50-100) — Automate, Ranch, Refine, Cool, Expand

  1. RANCHING — Grooming Station, Critter Drop-Off, Incubator.
  2. AUTOMATION — Automation Wire, Atmo/Hydro/Thermo Sensors.
  3. ADVANCED AUTOMATION — Logic Gates (AND/OR/NOT/Buffer/Filter), Automation Ribbon.
  4. SMELTING / REFINEMENT — Rock Crusher, Kiln, Metal Refinery.
  5. TEMPERATURE MODULATION — Thermo Sensor, Liquid Tepidizer, Thermo Aquatuner.
  6. INSULATION — Insulated Tile, Ceramic.
  7. (Optional, late) Solar / Natural Gas power, Rocketry/Space.

One-line summary: Farming → Power → Ventilation → Plumbing → Sanitation → (Super Computer) → Coal/Advanced Power → Improved Plumbing/Ventilation → Electrolyzer + Hydrogen Gen (SPOM) → Decor/Food/Showers → Ranching/Automation/Refinement/Cooling/Insulation.

4. Base Layout & Construction ^ top

This section is purely spatial — geometry, zoning, and routing. The detailed rules for what travels through the pipes/wires live in Power, Water, Gas, and Automation.

4.1 Core Geometry Rules

  • Make main rooms and hallways 4 TILES HIGH (fits decor/lights; most buildings are 1-3 tall)
  • Use 3-tile-wide vertical ladder/transit shafts: 1 ladder + Fire Pole + gap
  • When digging vertical shafts, leave 1-2 empty tiles on each side of the ladder for gas circulation and Dupe pathing
  • Minimize ladders early — Dupes are slow on ladders. Add FIRE POLES next to ladders (poles only go down)
  • Build wide rather than tall early; tall bases punish low-Athletics Dupes
  • Make spacious room entrances so traffic doesn't bottleneck

4.2 Vertical Zoning — Where Each Room Goes

Think in horizontal floors stacked around a central ladder/pole spine. Hot/dirty stuff goes far out to sides or down; oxygen sources go above so fresh O2 falls into rooms.

Top Floors (low traffic)

  • RESEARCH (Research Station / Super Computer)
  • SPOM / oxygen production & hydrogen handling — put oxygen sources ABOVE so cool O2 sinks down
  • High-tech parts STORAGE

Middle Floors (the living HUB — cluster buildings, same level)

  • GREAT HALL / MESS HALL (eating = morale; central so everyone reaches it)
  • KITCHEN / Electric Grill + Refrigerator (adjacent to hall)
  • RECREATION ROOM (near the hall)
  • BEDROOM / BARRACKS (adjacent but SEALED off from noise & traffic)

Lower-Mid Floors

  • REFRIGERATED FOOD + mineral/material STORAGE
  • WASHROOM block: TOILETS (Lavatory) + WASH BASIN/SINK + SHOWER

Bottom / Far-Side Floors (high heat, isolate behind insulated tiles)

  • POWER ROOM: Coal/other GENERATORS + BATTERIES
  • INDUSTRIAL: Metal Refinery, Kilns, heavy machinery
  • LIQUID/GAS plumbing reservoirs, Water Sieve, sewage processing

4.3 Community Standard: Floor Heights & Dimensions

The community consensus is 4 tiles high per functional room — this is the maximum height for most buildings and keeps everything reachable. Room widths: 16 tiles wide × 4 high = 64 tiles (fits Barracks, Latrine, Mess Hall) or 24 tiles wide × 4 high = 96 tiles (fits Power Plant, Greenhouse, Great Hall, Hospital).

Recommended: add a 2-tile utility gap between every floor. This gives a floor-to-floor pitch of 4 + 2 = 6 tiles total. All horizontal pipes, wires, pipe bridges, shutoffs, valves, and sensors run inside that gap — never inside the functional room above or below. This keeps rooms clean, hides decor-negative pipes from Dupes, and makes it far easier to add or modify lines later. The vertical transit shaft passes straight through the gap tiles.

VERTICAL FLOOR STACKING — 4-tile room + 2-tile utility gap (6-tile pitch)

  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |   ROOM (e.g. Barracks)                           |  row 4  ↑
  |   Buildings, Dupes, decor items live here        |  row 3  | 4 tiles
  |                                                  |  row 2  | (functional)
  |                                                  |  row 1  ↓
  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |  UTILITY GAP — top row: pipe runs, wire runs,    |  gap 2  ↑
  |    pipe bridges, shutoffs, valves, atmo sensors  |         | 2 tiles
  |  UTILITY GAP — bot row: ladders pass through     |  gap 1  ↓
  +--------------------------------------------------+
  |   ROOM (e.g. Mess Hall)                          |  rows 1-4 (4 tiles)
  +--------------------------------------------------+

RULE: Horizontal utility lines ONLY run in the gap. Rooms above and below stay
      clear of pipes and wires except for the final 1-tile connection to each building.

4.4 Community Horizontal Floor Pattern (top-down view)

From Klei forum and Steam discussions — the most widely used repeating pattern from left to right on each floor:

   ←—— 24 tiles ——→ ←3→ ←— 5 —→ ←— 16-24 tiles —→ ←3→ ←— 5 —→ ←—— 24 tiles ——→
   +----------------+   +-------+----------------+   +-------+----------------+
   |                |LAD|  MAINT|                |LAD|  MAINT|                |
   |  ROOM A        |POL|  ALCO |  ROOM B        |POL|  ALCO |  ROOM C        |
   |  (96 tiles)    |air|  VE   |  (64-96 tiles) |air|  VE   |  (96 tiles)    |
   +----------------+---+-------+----------------+---+-------+----------------+
   ROOM A/C = 24×4 = 96 tiles (large room)
   ROOM B   = 16×4 = 64 tiles (standard room)
   SHAFT    = 3 tiles: 1 ladder + 1 fire pole + 1 open air gap
   ALCOVE   = 5 tiles: transformer + pump + dispenser + spare space

The maintenance alcove (5 tiles wide) sits between a room and the shaft on each floor. It holds infrastructure — Power Transformer, pumps, dispensers — so those never clutter functional rooms. All floors repeat the same column positions, so risers run clean from top to bottom.

Gas Sorting by Floor

Gases sort by density automatically (full reference: 8.1). Design each floor so the right gas ends up where you want it without extra pumping:

   FLOOR     GAS PRESENT          DESIGN IMPLICATION
   ------    ---------            ------------------
   TOP       Hydrogen (rises)     → H2 generator / SPOM at top catches H2 passively
   UPPER     Oxygen (mid-weight)  → Bedrooms at upper-mid: O2 pools here naturally
   MIDDLE    Oxygen / Polluted O2 → Airflow/mesh tiles in long rooms (every ~8 tiles)
   LOWER     Carbon Dioxide       → CO2 pump near the floor of lower floors
   BOTTOM    CO2 + Chlorine       → Chlorine storage sealed at bottom; CO2 skimmer low

Use Airflow Tiles (mesh floor tiles) in long rooms (20+ tiles) — place 2–3 along the floor so O2 can reach the far end and CO2 can fall through to the lower floor. Normal solid tiles block vertical gas movement.

4.5 Per-Building Placement Notes

Generators

  • In a SEALED power room FAR from bedrooms — heat, CO2, and noise. Full power detail: Section 6.
  • CO2 sinks → put a CO2 pump beneath or let it pool away from breathing height
  • Keep coal storage right next to the gens

Toilets (Outhouse early → Lavatory later)

  • Group in a WASHROOM. A 4x8 room with ~4 toilets + 4 sinks handles a small to medium crew
  • Put a WASH BASIN/SINK on the EXIT tile so Dupes auto-wash hands
  • Keep the washroom NEAR (but sealed from) the kitchen

Food Hall + Kitchen

  • Central so all Dupes path through it; eating in a Great Hall is a big morale source
  • KITCHEN + REFRIGERATOR adjacent so cooked food travels minimal distance to tables
  • Keep the kitchen germ-clean: don't share air with toilets or polluted water

Bedroom / Barracks

  • SEALED and QUIET: away from generators, industry, and busy corridors
  • Put oxygen production above so O2 settles into the sleeping area

Waste Water

  • Route toilet/shower output to a sealed polluted-water reservoir, then a Water Sieve turns it back to clean water (loop detail: 7.3)
  • Keep polluted water sealed — it off-gasses Polluted Oxygen

4.6 Main Shaft & Utility Column — Tile-by-Tile

Key game fact: Each map cell has separate utility layers — a single tile can simultaneously hold ONE liquid pipe + ONE gas pipe + ONE power wire + ONE automation wire. You only add another column when you need a SECOND liquid or SECOND gas.

Early base main shaft: 2 liquids (clean/polluted) + 3 gases (O2/CO2/H2) → 2 conduit wall columns flanking the 3-tile transit core. The Heavi-Watt power backbone lives elsewhere (see 6.5).

The Transit Core (build this first, full height)

3 TILES WIDE: [ LADDER ] [ FIRE POLE ] [ open air ]
- Ladder = climb up.  Fire Pole = drop down fast.
- Open tile = Dupe pathing slack + gas circulation
- NO pipes embedded in the 3 transit tiles

The Conduit Walls (flank the core)

  • Build solid WALL tiles on each side of the transit core, then run pipes and standard/conductive wire THROUGH them (embedded inside the wall tile — invisible, walkway unaffected)
  • Group: CLEAN lines (clean water, oxygen, automation) on the living side; DIRTY lines (polluted water, CO2, hydrogen) on the maintenance side
  • Heavi-Watt is never embedded here — it has its own separate corridor (6.5)

Top-Down Cross-Section — Main Shaft (7 tiles wide)

   DIRTY / maintenance side  |   TRANSIT CORE   |   CLEAN / living side
        col1      col2       | col3  col4  col5 |  col6       col7
 +-----------+----------+----+----+-----+--------+-----------+
 |   WALL    |   WALL   |LAD |POLE| air |  WALL  |   WALL   |
 |  dirtyA   |  dirtyB  |    |    |     | cleanA |  cleanB  |
 +-----------+----------+----+----+-----+--------+-----------+

 col1 (dirtyA wall):  POLLUTED WATER (liq) + CARBON DIOXIDE (gas) + automation
 col2 (dirtyB wall):  spare/OIL (liq)      + HYDROGEN (gas)
 col3: LADDER    col4: FIRE POLE    col5: open air (path + gas)
 col6 (cleanA wall):  CLEAN WATER (liq)    + OXYGEN (gas) + automation
 col7 (cleanB wall):  spare (liq)          + CHLORINE / spare gas

 NOTE: Standard wire and Conductive wire CAN be embedded in col1/col2/col6/col7.
       Heavi-Watt backbone is NOT here — see 6.5.

Community Pattern: 5-Wide Shaft with Transit Tube

A wider variant used once transit tubes are unlocked. The extra two tiles add a transit tube column and a ledge for tube entry/exit.

5 TILES WIDE: [ LADDER ] [ FIRE POLE ] [ open air ] [ TRANSIT TUBE ] [ LEDGE ]
  The 3-wide core (cols 1-3) is built first; cols 4-5 are added when tubes unlock.
  No pipes run inside any of the 5 transit tiles.

Build Order for the Shaft

  1. Dig the 3-wide transit core the full intended height (cycle 1)
  2. Wall off col1 + col6 immediately (the two conduit wall columns); leave col2/col7 dug but empty
  3. Run clean water (col6) + polluted water (col1) risers in Phase 3
  4. Add O2 riser (col6 gas layer) at SPOM time (P4)
  5. Dig and wall off the separate Heavi-Watt corridor in the power/industrial zone at Phase 3; run the backbone and place transformers per floor at P4
  6. Add col2/col7 lines only when a 2nd liquid/gas appears (oil, chlorine, etc)

4.7 Routing the Spine — Vertical Risers + Horizontal Branches

Build ONE central vertical "utility column" beside the main transit shaft. All risers run UP/DOWN this column in parallel. On each floor, branch HORIZONTALLY off the column for the substances that floor needs.

Flow Direction in the Column

  • CLEAN WATER: source = sieve/reservoir at BOTTOM → rises UP → branches into SPOM (top), farms, kitchen, showers, research
  • OXYGEN: source = SPOM at TOP → falls DOWN → branches into every living floor
  • POLLUTED WATER: source = washroom (lower-mid) → drops DOWN → to Water Sieve at BOTTOM → output rejoins clean-water riser going back up
  • CARBON DIOXIDE: collected LOW → pumped DOWN/OUT to a pit or skimmer; never branch it up into living floors
  • POWER BACKBONE: separate hidden Heavi-Watt corridor (6.5); a Transformer taps off at each floor. Only standard/conductive + automation wire run in the column itself.

Text Diagram

[ TOP ]  Research ---+--- (clean water in, power)        SPOM/O2 (side)
                     |
[ MID ]  Great Hall / Kitchen / Rec / Bedrooms ---+--- (O2 vent, water, pwr)
                     |   (polluted water + CO2 risers pass THROUGH, no tap)
[ L-MID] Storage / Washroom ---+--- (clean water in; polluted water DOWN)
                     |
[ BOT ]  Power room / Industry / Water Sieve --far arm--> (CO2 out, pwr UP)
                     |
        ^CLEAN WATER & POWER rise UP   ^OXYGEN falls DOWN   ^P-WATER/CO2 go DOWN

4.8 Tapping Utilities from the Column to Each Facility

The Universal Tap Order (riser → building, in this sequence):

RISER ==[BRIDGE]==> [SHUTOFF] -> [VALVE] -> [branch pipe] -> BUILDING
  • BRIDGE first, ON THE RISER: keeps the riser feeding downstream floors first; a backed-up branch can't dam the column
  • SHUTOFF next: lets you cut one facility off instantly without touching the riser
  • VALVE last: caps the branch to exactly what the building consumes

Per-Utility Tap Rules

  • LIQUID: tap with a bridge; building OUTPUT onto the correct return riser; use a valve to protect a low-draw machine
  • GAS: branch to a GAS VENT placed HIGH in the room; add ATMO SENSOR + SHUTOFF set to ~1.8-2.0 kg/tile; one vent per room is usually enough
  • POWER: drop a POWER TRANSFORMER off the Heavi-Watt backbone at that floor; from the transformer output, run cheap 1k/2k wire into the room — the backbone itself never enters living areas
  • AUTOMATION: tap the signal line freely (no load limit); keep it on its OWN tile/layer

Split-Utility Facilities (e.g. Lavatory needs clean water IN, polluted OUT)

THE RULE: CROSS THE CLEAN LINE, NEVER THE DIRTY ONE.

  • Site the washroom against the dirty/maintenance side so the polluted DRAIN is a short hop onto the polluted riser
  • Bring ONLY the clean-water feed across the transit shaft (through the floor tile under the ladder)
  • For a multi-floor washroom stack: cross ONCE, not per floor

4.9 Layout Phasing — What to Place Where, Phase by Phase

Golden Rule: Claim the column line and floor heights on cycle 1. Each phase only FILLS reserved space — you never relocate the hub. These phases line up with the timeline in Section 3.

Phase 0 (Cycle 0-1) — Stake the Center

  • Dig a small living pocket around the printing pod (this floor = HUB)
  • COTS (1/Dupe), OUTHOUSE x1-2 + WASH BASIN (opposite side), RESEARCH STATION, STORAGE BIN
above:  [ . . . . reserved (O2/research) . . . . ]
HUB  :  [ cots | research | storage | outhouse+basin ]   <- pod floor
below:  [ . . . . reserved (power/plumbing) . . . . ]

Phase 1 (Cycle 1-3) — First Power & Air

  • MANUAL GENERATOR + BATTERY → floor BELOW the hub; OXYGEN DIFFUSER x1-2 → floor ABOVE (O2 sinks down)
ABOVE:  [ O2 diffuser x1-2 . . . reserved (SPOM/research) ]
HUB  :  [ cots | research | storage | outhouse+basin ]

5. Rooms ^ top

Rooms unlock bonuses only when ALL required buildings are present and the tile count is within the valid range. A room can only be ONE type — plan placement so buildings don't bleed across room boundaries.

5.1 Room Rules (Universal)

  • Tile count: counts every floor, wall, and ceiling tile enclosed within the room. Doors count as floor tiles. The room boundary is defined by solid tiles or doors — open space breaks it.
  • Room exclusivity: one room = one type. A Mess Table + Cot in the same room gives neither bonus. Keep room functions strictly separate.
  • Decor is evaluated from Dupe standing position — high-decor items on the ceiling or upper walls must be within 3 tiles of where Dupes stand to count toward Room eligibility.
  • Height matters for some rooms: Luxury Barracks and Private Bedroom need at least 4 tiles of ceiling height for the comfy bed to fit and the room to register.
  • Wild plants in Parks/Nature Reserves: must be untended (no farm tile / no Farmer's Touch). If a Dupe farms them, the plant is no longer "wild" and the room bonus is lost.
  • Doors: Mechanized Airlock counts as a wall tile (keeps room sealed). Manual Door also works but lets gas pass. Always use Mechanized Airlock for sealed rooms.

5.2 Build Priority by Game Phase

Phase 1–2 (Cycles 0–15) — Survival

  1. Latrine — stop Dupes from polluting the floor
  2. Barracks — morale +1, stops them sleeping on the floor
  3. Mess Hall — morale +3; biggest single morale gain available early

Phase 3 (Cycles 15–35) — Morale Upgrade

  1. Washroom — upgrade from Latrine; flush toilets + plumbed wash (+2)
  2. Great Hall — upgrade from Mess Hall once you have decor (+4 vs +3)
  3. Massage Clinic — doubles stress recovery; critical if stress is spiking

Phase 4–5 (Cycles 35–60) — Efficiency

  1. Power Plant — enables Power Control Station (+50% generator output)
  2. Greenhouse — enables Farmer's Touch (+20% crop yield)
  3. Recreation Room — gives Dupes a morale top-up between shifts

Phase 5–6 (Cycles 60+) — Luxury / Endgame

  1. Luxury Barracks or Private Bedrooms — morale +2/+3 respectively
  2. Hospital — replaces Massage Clinic when Dupes have diseases
  3. Nature Reserve — morale +6; expensive but the best morale item in the game
  4. Laboratory — +10% research speed; only worth building if you have 2+ active researchers

5.3 Sleeping Rooms

Barracks

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 4×4 to 8×5 | Required: 1+ Cot(s) | Morale: +1

  • Common mistake: putting a Cot in the same room as a Mess Table — the room is classified as Mess Hall and the Barracks bonus is lost.
  • Build tip: 4W×4H = 16 tiles, 2 cots side by side, mechanized airlock door. Minimum viable barracks — expand width as Dupe count grows.

  • Size: 12–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 8×5 min (needs 4-tile ceiling) | Required: 1+ Comfy Bed(s) + room decor ≥ 20 | Morale: +2

Private Bedroom

  • Size: 24–64 tiles | Practical W×H: 6×4 min, 8×5 recommended | Required: exactly 1 Comfy Bed + ALL background tiles Drywall | Morale: +3 morale, +200% stamina recovery
  • Placement: one room per Dupe — expensive in space. Build a row of identical 6×4 cells along a corridor, each with its own mechanized airlock.
  • Common mistake: leaving one exposed asteroid tile on the back wall — breaks the requirement even if all else is met. Check every tile.

5.4 Sanitation Rooms

Latrine

  • Build tip: a 4×4 room (16 tiles) fits 2 Outhouses + 1 Hand Sanitizer with room to walk.

6.2 Power Progression by Game Phase

Phase 1 (Cycles 0–10): Manual + Coal starter. Manual Generator for the Research Station and first Oxygen Diffusers. Transition to a Coal Generator (~600 W) as soon as you have coal and Combustion, with a battery buffer.

Phase 2 (Cycles 10–30): Coal bank + Smart Battery. Add Coal Generators as load grows. Install a Smart Battery immediately when the first coal gen goes in (see 11.2). Target: 2–4 Coal Generators feeding a Large Transformer per floor.

Phase 3 (~Cycle 30): The SPOM comes online. Its Hydrogen Generator produces 800 W and consumes all its own hydrogen — net zero cost from the coal grid. Its oxygen replaces all Diffusers; decommission those and reclaim their power.

Phase 4 (Cycles 40–70): Natural Gas. Tap a Natural Gas Geyser → Natural Gas Generator (800 W) for free renewable power. One average geyser sustains 1–2 generators; buffer with a Gas Reservoir.

Phase 5 (Cycles 70+): Petroleum / Steam Turbines. Petroleum Generator (2,000 W) is the highest single-building output. Steam Turbines pair well with volcanic geysers and output cooled water. Combine: Coal → Hydrogen → Natural Gas → Petroleum as each tier comes online.

Grid design rules:

6.5 Heavi-Watt Wire Rules

This is the single canonical home for the Heavi-Watt rules — the base layout (4.6/4.7) refers back here.

  • Heavi-Watt Wire CANNOT be embedded inside a solid tile (unlike normal wire). It must sit in an open air cell.
  • It carries a −25 decor penalty over a 6-tile radius. The total affected zone spans 13 tiles wide (6 left + wire tile + 6 right). Any Dupe who sees or paths within it loses decor → stress. Keep it completely hidden.
  • Run it through a dedicated 1-tile-wide sealed corridor: wall tile on each side, wire in the empty cell between. No Dupe access, no windows.
  • To pass through a wall tile, use a Heavi-Watt Joint Plate (replaces the wall tile; lets the wire cross).
  • Place a Power Transformer at each floor tap to convert Heavi-Watt into standard wire before it enters any room.
  • Run the corridor on the power/industrial side of the base — never next to bedrooms, the Great Hall, or the main transit shaft.

6.7 Common Power Mistakes

  • Running generators without a Smart Battery: they run 24/7 burning all fuel. Smart Battery automation cuts fuel use 50–80% (11.2).
  • Battery on the consumer side of a Transformer: it only buffers that circuit's cap (1,000–4,000 W), not the whole base. Put main batteries on the backbone side.
  • Routing Heavi-Watt wire near living areas: the −25 decor hits every room within 6 tiles. It must be in a sealed, hidden corridor.
  • Overloading one circuit: a Coal Gen (600 W) + two Diffusers (120 W ea) + Research Station (100 W) = 940 W — nearly at 1,000 W. One more building trips it. Watch the Power Overlay.
  • Building a Power Plant room with Coal Generators: they don't qualify. Use a Natural Gas Gen, Petroleum Gen, or Steam Turbine.
  • Placing Coal Generators near bedrooms: 4 kDTU/s heat + CO2. Seal them in an insulated room at the bottom/far side.
  • Forgetting Heavi-Watt needs a Joint Plate to cross a wall tile: skip it and the backbone stops at the wall.

7.5 Storage & Reservoirs

  • Liquids pool by density in open tiles. Keep clean and polluted water in SEPARATE sealed pits.
  • A Liquid Reservoir holds 5,000 kg; set an automation sensor to stop the pump when full.
  • For bulk storage, flood a sealed room. A 5×5 room holds ~25,000 kg of water. Leave a small gas pocket above flooded storage or the pump can stall.
  • Store WASTE water SEALED and away from breathing areas — it off-gasses Polluted Oxygen.
  • Keep a few cycles of clean-water RESERVE so a sieve hiccup doesn't dry out farms. Insulate temperature-critical storage.

7.6 Tapping Geysers

Geysers erupt in cycles: active period (high flow) then dormant period (no flow). Always build a buffer reservoir to cover dormant gaps.

  • Analyze first: use the Geyser Analyzer (needs a Science Dupe). Read average kg/s, eruption/dormancy rate, and temperature.
  • Water Geyser tap: seal a room around it, place a Liquid Pump, pipe to a reservoir, add a Hydro Sensor to stop the pump when full.
  • Cooling hot water: route through a Thermo Aquatuner (polluted water or oil coolant) to <70°C before use.
  • Cool Steam Vent: vent into a sealed steam chamber; place a Steam Turbine above to condense it into water + generate power.

7.7 Temperature & Heat in Liquids

  • Liquids transfer heat to whatever they touch. Hot Water Geyser output (95°C) will heat pipes and the base if uncooled.
  • Thermo Aquatuner: 1,200 W; drops liquid temperature by exactly 14°C per pass; extracted heat goes to the surroundings. Run it in an insulated room with a high-thermal-mass liquid (Petroleum or Brine).
  • Insulated Pipe (Igneous Rock, Granite, or Abyssalite) minimizes transit heat exchange. Use it through inhabited areas and cold biomes.
  • Water freezes at 0°C (burst pipe in cold biomes — insulate) and boils at 100°C. Keep hot loops below 95°C.

7.8 Common Liquid Mistakes

  • No water loop / no Water Sieve: you run out of sand and flush polluted water away. Build the Lavatory→Sieve→Reservoir loop by Cycle 5.
  • Liquid Pump not submerged: a pump in a dry tile does nothing (mini = 1 tile, standard = 2 tiles).
  • Running out of sand: the Sieve eats 1 kg sand/kg water (~75 kg/cycle for 5 Dupes). Stock >2,000 kg; crush Regolith into Sand.
  • Hot water in uninsulated pipes through a cold biome: it freezes mid-pipe and bursts. Always use Insulated Pipe in cold biomes.
  • Polluted water open to air above 25°C: emits PO2, contaminating the base. Seal it or keep it cold.
  • Ignoring geyser dormancy: a 2 kg/s average geyser still hits zero output. Size a buffer for the longest dormancy period.

8.4 CO2 Management

CO2 sinks to the floor. Unmanaged, it suffocates Dupes working at floor level (Mining, Farming, Generator rooms).

  • CO2 Pocket: dig a sealed pit 2–4 tiles deep at the base of the main shaft. CO2 sinks in naturally. No power; just keep Dupes out.
  • Carbon Skimmer: 120 W; needs 1 kg/s polluted water; absorbs CO2 in its tile, outputs polluted water. Place at floor level.
  • Algae Distiller: converts CO2 + Water → Algae + Polluted Water. 80 W; useful early.
  • Vent to space: mid/late game — pump CO2 up a sealed corridor and vent at the surface.

CO2 room rule: always place a Gas Pump + pipe in any generator room and route CO2 to a dedicated pit.

8.5 Oxygen Distribution

  • Oxygen Diffuser (early): 120 W; 41.67 g/s O2; consumes Algae (100 g/s). Algae is finite — temporary only.
  • SPOM (Cycle 25–35): Electrolyzer-based, self-powered. Replace all Diffusers (Section 12).
  • Distribution: pump SPOM oxygen through pipes to a Gas Vent placed HIGH in each room. For bases >5 floors, pipe to one vent per floor rather than relying on diffusion. One vent per room is usually enough.
  • Overpressure: a Gas Vent stops at >1.8 kg/tile. Add an Atmo Sensor + shutoff (11.3). Use High-Pressure Vents only in sealed rooms designed for it.

8.6 Filters & Reservoirs

  • Gas Filter: critical in SPOMs to separate H2 from O2 — place on the Electrolyzer output, set target = Hydrogen. H2 exits the secondary port; everything else passes through.
  • Gas Reservoir: buffer between a geyser and its consumer (Natural Gas Geyser → Reservoir → Generator). A 150 kg reservoir covers ~5 minutes of generator runtime at full output.
  • Natural Gas Geyser tap: seal the room, Gas Pump at the top of the pocket (natural gas rises), pipe to a reservoir.

8.7 Special Gases

Chlorine — extremely toxic; never allow into inhabited areas. Seal a small room with Chlorine, place a Seed Safe inside: it kills all germs passively. A Chlorine Geyser gives infinite supply for germ control.

Natural Gas — rises (lower density than O2). Seal the top of the generator room so it accumulates there, reducing pumping. One geyser (~100–200 g/s) sustains one generator (needs 90 g/s).

Steam — only useful in a Steam Turbine (needs ≥125°C; outputs cooled water ~95°C). Otherwise it's waste heat in gas form.

8.8 Common Gas Mistakes

  • CO2 pooling at floor level unnoticed: Dupes pass out "suffocating." Check the Gas Overlay; add a Carbon Skimmer or CO2 pit.
  • Gas Pump in the wrong position: it pumps the tile directly above its intake. Put it where the target gas is densest (CO2 at the floor, H2 at the ceiling).
  • SPOM not sealed before startup: H2 drifts into living areas. Seal all walls before activating the Electrolyzer.
  • Gas Vent overpressure: the vent stops at >1.8 kg/tile. Expand the room or reduce vent output.
  • No buffer reservoir on a Natural Gas line: the generator loses output during dormancy. Add a reservoir.
  • Letting Chlorine escape: even one tile in a Dupe path incapacitates. Seal Chlorine rooms with solid tiles.

  • [ ] Cycle 5–15: Dig a CO2 pit at the base of the main shaft. Route generator-room CO2 there.
  • [ ] Cycle 10–20: Deodorizers (120 W, Sand) near any PO2 source.
  • [ ] Cycle 20–35: Build SPOM. Seal it. Verify H2 → generator, O2 → base vents. Decommission Diffusers.

11. Automation ^ top

Automation wire carries a GREEN (on) / RED (off) signal. It has no power load and no capacity limit — a completely separate layer from power wire. One automation wire can run the full height of the base. The key is placing sensors next to the things they measure and keeping the signal on its own tile layer.

11.1 Signal Wire Basics & Routing

  • Automation wire CAN be embedded inside a solid wall tile (like normal/conductive wire) — route it through the conduit wall columns alongside the pipes.
  • Run one automation riser in each conduit column (dirty + clean side). Branch horizontally to each floor's sensors and shutoffs, the same way you branch pipes.
  • Keep automation wire on its OWN tile layer — never share a cell with power wire. In a conduit wall, the stack is: liquid pipe + gas pipe + power wire + automation wire, all in one tile.
  • Signals flow FROM sensors TO devices (pumps, shutoffs, generators). A sensor output that triggers multiple devices needs a wire to each, or a splitter.

11.2 Power Automation — Smart Battery + Generator Control

This is the #1 automation every base needs — build it the moment coal generators go in (Phase 3).

[Smart Battery]
  LOW threshold (~5%)  → sends GREEN → all Generators turn ON
  HIGH threshold (~99%) → sends RED   → all Generators turn OFF
  • Wire: Smart Battery automation output → each Generator's automation input port. Result: generators only burn fuel when the battery actually needs charging.
  • Set LOW 5% / HIGH 99% to avoid rapid flicker. Add a BUFFER Gate (5–10 s delay) if they still flicker.
  • Stagger multiple generators: use separate Smart Batteries with different thresholds — Gen 1 at 20%, Gen 2 at 10%, Gen 3 at 5% — so the cleanest/freest fires first and the dirtiest (coal) fires last.
  • Hydrogen Generator (SPOM): control with a separate Smart Battery inside the SPOM room so it only fires when the SPOM's own battery is low. Don't tie it to the main bank or it fires constantly and wastes hydrogen.
  • Natural Gas Generator: Gas Reservoir + Hydro/pressure sensor → shut off below a safe gas threshold.
  • Steam Turbine: Thermo Sensor on the steam input — run above 125°C, off when exhausted.

11.3 Gas / Oxygen Automation — Atmo Sensor + Shutoff

Place one per room that receives piped oxygen (and on the SPOM's hydrogen handling).

  • Atmo Sensor reads the room's total gas pressure (kg/tile). Set GREEN (vent open) when pressure < ~1.8 kg/tile; RED (vent closed) when ≥ 2.0 kg/tile.
  • Wire: Atmo Sensor → Gas Shutoff on the branch feeding that room's vent. When the room is full, the shutoff closes and the oxygen riser continues to the next floor without backing up.
  • Placement: put the Atmo Sensor AWAY from the vent outlet so it reads averaged room pressure, not a local spike.
  • CO2 removal: an Atmo Sensor set to a CO2 filter near the floor → gas pump shutoff, so the pump only runs when CO2 is present.

11.4 Liquid Automation — Hydro Sensor + Shutoff

  • Hydro Sensor reads liquid mass flowing past on a pipe.
  • Reservoir overflow protection: Hydro Sensor on the reservoir output → RED (pump off) when full → wire to the input pump's shutoff.
  • Water Sieve feed: Hydro Sensor on the polluted feed → if flow drops to 0 (reservoir empty), RED to the sieve's power shutoff so it doesn't run dry and waste sand.
  • SPOM water supply: Hydro Sensor before the Electrolyzer → if clean water stops, RED to the SPOM's gas pump shutoff (12.10).
  • Farm irrigation: the Farm Tile's own sensor triggers irrigation only when the plant needs it, saving water.

11.5 Logic Gates — Combining Signals

Use logic gates when a device should activate based on TWO+ conditions. Build these mid-game (Phase 6) once basics run.

  • AND — GREEN only when ALL inputs are GREEN. E.g. run a pump only when the reservoir is full AND it's day shift.
  • OR — GREEN when ANY input is GREEN. E.g. alert if oxygen pressure OR CO2 level is out of range.
  • NOT — inverts the signal. E.g. a sensor that goes GREEN when full → NOT → RED (close) to a shutoff.
  • BUFFER — delays a GREEN by N seconds. Use on generator control to prevent flicker.
  • FILTER — passes GREEN only if it stays GREEN for N seconds (ignores brief spikes).

11.6 What to Build, by Phase

  • Phase 1-2: none needed; a manual generator doesn't benefit from smart control.
  • Phase 3 (Cycle 10-30): Smart Battery → Coal Generator (the most impactful automation). Add a Hydro Sensor on the Water Sieve feed.
  • Phase 4 (~Cycle 30, SPOM): Atmo Sensors inside the SPOM + Hydro Sensor on the water feed — required for a stable SPOM.
  • Phase 4-5: Atmo Sensor + Shutoff on every oxygen branch (one per room).
  • Phase 5-6: Thermo Sensors on heat sources → cooling. Hydro Sensors on all reservoirs.
  • Phase 6: Logic gates (AND/OR/NOT/BUFFER/FILTER) — only when a runaway problem needs them.

12. SPOM — Self-Powered Oxygen Machine ^ top

12.1 What is a SPOM?

A SPOM is a sealed room with an Electrolyzer and a Hydrogen Generator wired so the hydrogen the Electrolyzer makes fuels the generator, and the generator powers the Electrolyzer. It completely replaces Oxygen Diffusers and Algae Terrariums by ~Cycle 25–35 — the single most important mid-game infrastructure upgrade.

Key placement rules:

  • Place the Gas Pump near the Electrolyzer. The Gas Filter does the separation, not gravity alone.
  • The Hydrogen Generator goes in the same sealed room OR connects by a dedicated H2 pipe — never vent H2 into open space.
  • The room needs ≥20 tiles of free gas space so it doesn't immediately overpressure and stall the Electrolyzer (stalls above 2 kg/tile).
  • Use Insulated Tiles on all walls and floor — the Electrolyzer outputs 4 kDTU/s at 70°C.

12.6 Water Supply

  • Requirement: 1 kg/s per Electrolyzer, indefinitely — a permanent, constant drain.
  • Sources: a Water Geyser averaging ≥1 kg/s, a Cool Steam Vent condensed via Steam Turbine, or accumulated ice melt. The Lavatory waste loop is NOT enough — its surplus is too small to feed an Electrolyzer continuously.
  • Temperature: the Electrolyzer accepts any temperature, but colder water (20°C vs 95°C geyser) heats the room less. Pre-cool with a Thermo Aquatuner if heat is a concern.
  • Pipe in: a Liquid Valve set to 1 kg/s is enough. Add a Liquid Shutoff on automation tied to the Smart Battery or a sensor to stop input if the room overpressures (>2 kg/tile stalls the Electrolyzer).
  • Add a Liquid Reservoir (5,000 kg) on the input to buffer through geyser dormancy.

12.7 Power Wiring

Option A — Internal self-contained circuit (recommended):

  • Wire the Hydrogen Generator → Electrolyzer + Gas Pump + Gas Filter on an internal loop inside the SPOM room. Add a Smart Battery to this internal circuit to control when the generator runs.
  • Export surplus through a Transformer to the Heavi-Watt backbone. Benefit: the SPOM keeps producing O2 even if the external grid fails.

Option B — Shared grid: wire the generator into the backbone and pull power back for the Electrolyzer. Simpler, but if the grid trips the Electrolyzer stops. Not recommended for >5 dupes.

12.9 Startup Procedure

  1. Build the room first — Insulated Tiles on all walls/ceiling/floor, completely sealed. Verify with the Gas Overlay.
  2. Build all internal buildings and pipes/wires inside the sealed room.
  3. Connect the water pipe through a wall (pipes pass through tiles without breaking the seal). Add a Liquid Shutoff outside.
  4. Wire the internal power circuit (HG → Smart Battery → Electrolyzer + Gas Pump + Gas Filter; Transformer to export). Do NOT power the Electrolyzer from the external grid yet.
  5. Pre-fill the Smart Battery to 100% from a temporary external source.
  6. Open the water valve. The Electrolyzer starts; H2 and O2 pressurize the room.
  7. Wait for H2 pressure — the Gas Pump moves gas through the Filter; H2 reaches the generator.
  8. Hydrogen Generator fires (battery below 100%) and now powers the Electrolyzer. Disconnect external power.
  9. Verify O2 vents to base and no H2 reaches living areas (Gas Overlay).

12.10 Automation & Sensors

  • Smart Battery (primary): LOW 10% (generator fires) / HIGH 100% (stops). H2 isn't wasted burning continuously.
  • Gas pressure on the H2 pipe (optional): if <100 g, close a Gas Shutoff to the generator so it doesn't run on insufficient fuel.
  • Room pressure → water shutoff (recommended): an Atmo/pressure sensor in the room; if >1.8 kg, stop water input so the SPOM doesn't flood when overpressured.
  • General gas/liquid automation patterns: 11.3 / 11.4.

12.11 Common SPOM Mistakes

  • Not sealing the room before activation: the most common disaster — H2 drifts into the base and displaces O2. Seal first, always.
  • Skipping the Gas Filter: O2 and H2 mix in the output pipe; the generator can't burn the mix and the SPOM stops being self-powered.
  • Using a large Gas Pump instead of a mini: 240 W vs 60 W — wastes ~22% of the generator output for no benefit.
  • Connecting the Electrolyzer to the base grid directly: a grid trip halts O2 production. Use the internal Smart Battery circuit (Option A).
  • Forgetting the Electrolyzer stalls above 2 kg/tile: ensure the Gas Pump is actively running. Overpressure is the #1 cause of a "SPOM that suddenly stopped."
  • No water buffer reservoir: when the geyser goes dormant the SPOM runs the pipes dry within minutes. Add a 5,000 kg reservoir.
  • Building too close to living areas: H2 Generator emits 4 kDTU/s heat + −10 decor through walls. Put the SPOM at the top of the base, isolated.
  • Not researching both Improved Plumbing AND Renewable Energy: both are required. Don't delay Renewable Energy past Cycle 20.

13. Skills, Morale & Stress ^ top

Skills / Leveling

  • Dupes earn Skill Points by working; spend them in the Skill Tree (12 skill groups, 32 total skills).
  • Match skills to a Dupe's INTEREST: -1 effective morale cost and faster work.
  • DIGGING is one of the most valuable early skills.

Morale vs Stress (the make-or-break system)

  • Every skill a Dupe learns RAISES their required morale by that skill's tier.
  • If morale < required morale, STRESS climbs fast until the Dupe "breaks." Stress overload is the #1 cause of colony collapse.
  • DON'T over-train Dupes early. Save high-tier jobs until AFTER you've built morale sources.
  • Build morale BEFORE you buy expensive skills. Morale sources: food quality (9.5), rooms (Section 5), decor, recreation, sleep + downtime.

Give Dupes a proper sleep + downtime schedule block; tweak the colony schedule so work, eat, recreation, and sleep don't collide into bottlenecks.

14. Misc — Digging & Storage ^ top

Digging (Excavation Efficiency)

  • Prioritize a DIGGER Dupe (interest in Excavation): faster digging, cheap morale.
  • Set priority 9 on urgent tiles (escape routes, the next room).
  • DON'T breach unknown biome walls early — they hide hot gases, germs, and floods.
  • Watch what falls: digging from UNDER liquids/sand causes spills/cave-ins; dig from the side or top.
  • Abyssalite and dense rock dig slowly — route around them early.
  • Set STORAGE so haulers tidy debris automatically (loose debris lowers decor and slows pathing).

Storage per Facility

  • GENERAL: storage = SHORT HAULS. Put each material near where it's consumed; whitelist with a filter.
  • POWER ROOM: Storage Bin set to COAL beside the generators.
  • KITCHEN / FOOD HALL: Refrigerator (or Ration Box early) next to cook + tables; separate bin for raw ingredients. Sealed sterile air slows spoilage.
  • FARM: small bin for seeds/fertilizer near planters; bin for harvested crop feeding the kitchen.
  • RANCH: feeder bin inside the stable; egg/meat bin near the drop-off.
  • INDUSTRY / REFINERY: ore bins beside the refinery; output bin nearby. Keep far from the living base.
  • MATERIAL/GENERAL: central-lower storage room for raw rock/ore; one filter per bin.
  • Mark loose debris to Sweep; set storage priority so idle Dupes tidy automatically. Storing solids in a vacuum or cold room stops food rot and heat leaking (late-game trick).
